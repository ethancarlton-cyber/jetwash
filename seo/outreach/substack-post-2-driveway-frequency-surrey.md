Most homeowners in Surrey clean their driveway when it finally looks bad enough to bother them. That is usually a year or two too late. By the time green algae or dark black spots are obvious from the pavement, the growth has already established a root system, and on block paving the jointing sand has often washed away.

So how often should you actually be cleaning outdoor surfaces in this part of the country? The honest answer is that it depends on the surface, the aspect, and how much shade and tree cover the property has. Here is a practical guide based on what we see across Redhill, Reigate, Dorking and the wider RH1 area.

## Why Surrey is harder on surfaces than most of the UK

The South East has a damp, mild climate, and the North Downs belt around Reigate, Dorking and Banstead sits under more rainfall and humidity than the national average. Mild winters mean moss and algae never really stop growing, and limited winter sunlight means north and west-facing surfaces stay wet for days at a time. Add the mature trees that shade so many Surrey gardens, and you have close to ideal conditions for biological growth on driveways, patios and render.

That is why a cleaning schedule that works in a drier, sunnier region will leave a Surrey driveway looking tired well before its next clean is due.

## Cleaning frequency by surface

**Driveways and paths: every 12 to 18 months.** Shaded driveways, or any under trees, are closer to the 12-month end. Sunny, open, sealed driveways can stretch to two or three years. Block paving benefits from re-sanding after each clean to keep the joints stable and slow down weed regrowth.

**Patios: every 12 to 18 months,** or annually if the patio is prone to black spot (lichen). Indian sandstone and other natural stone patios, which are common across Surrey, are especially prone to it in damp, shaded gardens.

**Rendered walls: every two to three years,** depending on exposure. North and west-facing walls stay damp longer and green up faster, so they usually need doing more often than a south-facing front.

**Decking: annually,** ideally in spring before the outdoor season. Wooden decking becomes genuinely slippery with algae over winter, so this one is as much about safety as appearance.

## Five signs it is already overdue

You do not need to track dates if you know what to look for. Book a clean when you notice any of these:

1. A green tinge visible on the surface when it is dry, not just when wet. Dry-visible green means the growth is well established.
2. Weeds coming back in block paving joints within a month of pulling them. That means the jointing sand is depleted and needs professional restoration.
3. Dark oil patches where you park that are spreading rather than staying put.
4. You cannot remember the original colour of the paving. Block paving fades to a uniform grey-brown as grime builds up, and the change is so gradual that most people only notice when a neighbour cleans theirs.
5. You are about to sell, rent or remortgage. A clean approach and driveway is one of the cheapest ways to lift kerb appeal before viewings.

## Why regular beats occasional

The counter-intuitive part is that cleaning more often is cheaper per year, not more expensive. A driveway maintained on a 12 to 18 month cycle takes less time to clean each visit because the growth never gets a proper hold. Leave it for three or four years and you are into heavy treatment, and in the worst cases the moss and lichen have started to break down the surface itself. Replacing a block paving driveway runs into thousands of pounds. Regular cleaning is a few hundred.

## The bottom line

For most Surrey homeowners, an annual or 18-monthly exterior clean keeps surfaces looking their best and prevents the slow damage that leads to expensive remedial work. Spring is the priority season, since winter moss has peaked but not yet hardened. If you are on a tighter budget, prioritise the surfaces people actually see from the street: the driveway, the front path and any visible render.
