# Research Asset Outreach - Same Day JetWash (2026-07-13)

This complements, not replaces, `CITATION-BLITZ-SHEET.md` (directory/citation submissions) and `LOCAL-OUTREACH-PACK.md` (trade + community reciprocal-link emails). Those are NAP/citation and local-trade plays. This sheet is different: it is the specific, named people and publications who would plausibly cite one of the 4 new research pages because the data is genuinely useful to their audience.

**Do not start outreach until all 4 pages are live on samedayjetwash.com and indexed in Google Search Console.** Sending journalists/societies/bloggers a link to a page that 404s or is not yet indexable burns the contact and the credibility.

All targets below were confirmed to exist via live web search on 2026-07-13. Nothing here is invented. Where a contact route was not confirmed, it says so rather than guessing an email address.

## 4. Paved Front Gardens & Flood Risk

**Targets (12):**

- **RHS (Royal Horticultural Society)** — created the "3 million gardens paved since 2005" flood-risk narrative this report builds on. rhs.org.uk/garden-design/permeable-paving and rhs.org.uk/science/gardening-in-a-changing-world/front-garden-research. Pitch: a Surrey-specific data cut extends their own campaign with fresh regional evidence. Contact: general enquiries via rhs.org.uk contact form.
- **Surrey Wildlife Trust** — county wildlife charity, runs "Nature-based Solutions" flood-risk work. surreywildlifetrust.org, Nature-based Solutions / Wetlands pages. Pitch: directly supports their nature-based flood mitigation messaging to local residents. Contact: press.office@surreywt.org.uk or info@surreywt.org.uk.
- **National Flood Forum** — UK charity supporting flood-affected communities, runs 300+ local flood action groups. nationalfloodforum.org.uk, Resources/"our services" pages. Pitch: a plain-English explainer of the 2008 rule and permeable paving is exactly the practical prevention content they signpost to homeowners. Contact: helpline 01299 403055, or site contact form.
- **The Flood Hub** — Environment Agency/United Utilities-backed advice hub, runs the "Unpave the Way" campaign. thefloodhub.co.uk/unpave-the-way. - **National Park City Front Gardens (Ealing Front Gardens Project)** — campaign blog tracking loss of front gardens to paving. frontgardens.nationalparkcity.org, Blog/Inspiration sections. Pitch: they actively publish flood-risk-and-paving explainers, a data-heavy regional report is native content for them. Contact: general enquiries via nationalparkcity.org (no direct email captured).
- **susdrain (CIRIA)** — UK's community website for Sustainable Drainage Systems, run by CIRIA, hosts an existing "Unpave the Way" case study. susdrain.org, Case studies/resources pages. - **Surrey County Council — Flood Risk Management** — Lead Local Flood Authority for Surrey, publishes the Local Flood Risk Management Strategy 2017-2032. surreycc.gov.uk (flooding / SuDS pre-application planning advice sections). Pitch: a citizen-facing explainer of the 5m² permitted-development rule supports their own "prevent surface water at source" objective. Contact: general council contact form.
- **Reigate & Banstead Borough Council** — covers Redhill/Reigate, documented flooding in the 2013 storms and a 2015 Section 19 investigation. reigate-banstead.gov.uk/info/20061/flooding.
