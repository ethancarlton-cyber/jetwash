As a professional pressure washing operator serving residential properties across Surrey, I can share current pricing data that may be useful for your piece.

Professional driveway cleaning in the UK typically costs between £4 and £9 per square metre, depending on surface type and condition. A block paving driveway that's maintained annually costs a few hundred pounds per clean. Replacing that same driveway because of neglected maintenance costs thousands.

The most cost-effective approach is prevention: regular professional cleaning is significantly cheaper than remedial work or surface replacement.

## Template 3: Seasonal Home Maintenance

**Use for queries about:** spring cleaning, seasonal home maintenance, preparing your home for summer/winter, annual property maintenance checklist

---

**Subject line:** Seasonal pressure washing advice for UK homeowners

As a professional pressure washer serving properties across Surrey, here's what I'd recommend for seasonal exterior maintenance.

**Spring (March-May)** is the best time for a comprehensive exterior clean. Winter leaves driveways and patios covered in moss, algae, and leaf debris. Cleaning in spring removes everything that's accumulated over the wet months and gives you clean outdoor surfaces for summer use. It's also when decking should be cleaned — wooden decking becomes dangerously slippery with algae growth over winter.

**Autumn (September-October)** is ideal for a secondary clean or targeted maintenance. Clearing leaf debris from driveways and paths before winter prevents staining and reduces the organic matter that fuels moss growth.

**Avoid cleaning in freezing conditions.** Water can freeze in cracks and joints, potentially causing surface damage. Most professional operators pause work when temperatures drop below 3-4 degrees Celsius.

For homeowners who clean annually, spring is the priority season. For those on an 18-month cycle, alternating between spring and autumn works well.

One tip that saves homeowners money: address oil stains and specific problem areas promptly rather than waiting for the annual clean. Fresh oil stains are far easier and cheaper to remove than old, deeply embedded ones.
