# 5 Signs Your Driveway Needs Professional Pressure Washing

*Last updated: February 2026*

Your driveway is one of the first things people see when they visit your home. It's also one of the most exposed surfaces on your property, taking a beating from vehicles, weather, and organic growth year after year. But how do you know when it's time to call in a professional?

Here are five clear signs that your driveway is overdue for a proper clean.

## 1. Green or Black Discolouration Has Taken Over

If your driveway has a noticeable green tinge or dark patches spreading across the surface, that's algae and moss establishing itself. In Surrey's damp climate, this happens faster than you might expect, particularly on north-facing driveways or those shaded by trees and hedges.

Algae isn't just unsightly. On smooth surfaces like tarmac or sealed block paving, it creates a dangerously slippery film, especially when wet. Moss growing in block paving joints can also gradually displace the jointing sand, weakening the structure of the paving over time.

A professional pressure wash removes all organic growth and restores the original surface colour. Most operators use biocide pre-treatment to kill algae at the root, which slows regrowth and keeps your driveway cleaner for longer.

**When to act:** If the green is visible when the surface is dry, it's already well established and needs professional treatment.

## 2. Weeds Are Growing Through the Joints

Block paving is extremely popular across Surrey, and one of its biggest maintenance challenges is weed growth between the blocks. Once weeds establish root systems in the jointing sand, they spread quickly and become increasingly difficult to remove by hand.

Pulling weeds out manually often leaves roots behind, and the problem returns within weeks. Domestic weedkillers help temporarily but don't address the underlying issue: the jointing sand has been depleted, leaving gaps where seeds germinate.

Professional pressure washing blasts weeds out completely, roots and all. A good service will also offer re-sanding afterwards, filling the joints with fresh kiln-dried sand that inhibits future weed growth and stabilises the blocks.

**When to act:** If you're pulling weeds from your driveway more than once a month, it's time for professional cleaning and re-sanding.

## 3. Oil Stains Are Spreading

Every car drips small amounts of oil, and over time these drips accumulate into noticeable dark patches. On block paving and concrete, oil soaks into the porous surface and becomes progressively harder to remove the longer it sits.

Domestic pressure washers rarely have the power or heat to shift embedded oil stains. Professional equipment uses heated water at 3000+ PSI combined with specialist degreasing agents that break down oil before it's blasted away.

Fresh oil stains (less than a few months old) are almost always fully removable. Older, deeply penetrated stains may lighten significantly but not disappear completely, particularly on very porous surfaces.

**When to act:** The sooner oil stains are treated, the better the result. If you can see a dark patch spreading where you park, don't wait.

## 4. The Original Colour Has Disappeared

This is the most common sign, and it happens so gradually that many homeowners don't notice until they see a freshly cleaned neighbouring driveway for comparison.

Block paving that was once vibrant red or charcoal fades to a uniform grey-brown. Concrete darkens. Tarmac loses its rich black colour. Natural stone patios look dull and lifeless.

In most cases, the original colour is still there underneath layers of dirt, atmospheric pollution, algae, and general grime. A thorough professional clean reveals the surface you forgot you had.

The transformation is often dramatic. Before-and-after results from professional pressure washing consistently surprise homeowners who assumed their driveway was just "old" when it was simply dirty.

**When to act:** If you can't remember what colour your driveway used to be, or if a neighbour's freshly cleaned drive makes yours look shabby, it's time.

## 5. You're Preparing to Sell or Let Your Property

First impressions matter enormously in property sales. Estate agents consistently report that kerb appeal, the attractiveness of a property from the street, significantly influences buyer perception and can add 5-10% to perceived property value.

A dirty, moss-covered driveway and stained patio send the wrong message before a buyer even steps through the door. Professional pressure washing is one of the most cost-effective ways to improve kerb appeal. For a fraction of the cost of interior decoration, you can make the entire exterior look well-maintained and cared for.

For landlords, a clean exterior helps attract quality tenants and justifies asking rents. For homeowners selling, it's one of the simplest upgrades with the highest return on investment.

**When to act:** Book a professional clean 1-2 weeks before your property goes on the market or before viewings begin. The results last several months, giving you a clean exterior throughout the selling process.

## How Often Should You Clean Your Driveway?

As a general rule in Surrey's climate, professional driveway cleaning every 12-18 months keeps surfaces in excellent condition. Properties in shaded areas, under trees, or in particularly damp locations may benefit from annual cleaning.

Regular maintenance is also more cost-effective in the long run. A driveway cleaned every year requires less intensive treatment each time, keeping per-visit costs lower and preventing long-term damage from embedded organic growth.
