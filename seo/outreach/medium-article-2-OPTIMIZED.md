A dirty driveway doesn't just look bad — it can reduce your property's perceived value by up to 10%, create dangerous slip hazards, and cause long-term surface damage that costs thousands to repair. Here are five clear signs that your driveway needs professional attention, not just a hose down.

These signs are particularly relevant for homeowners in Surrey and the South East, where the damp climate accelerates moss, algae, and biological growth on outdoor surfaces.

## Key Facts: When to Clean Your Driveway

- Professional cleaning needed every **12–18 months** in the UK climate
- Green algae growth becomes a **slip hazard** when wet (HSE reports slips cause 30%+ of domestic injuries)
- Block paving with depleted sand joints deteriorates **3x faster** than sealed paving
- Oil stains penetrate porous surfaces within **24–48 hours** — act fast for best results
- A clean driveway adds **£5,000–£10,000** to perceived property value (estate agent estimates)

## 1. Green or Black Discolouration Has Taken Over

If your driveway has a visible green tinge or dark patches spreading across the surface, that's algae and moss establishing itself. In Surrey's damp climate, this happens faster than most homeowners expect — particularly on north-facing driveways or those shaded by mature trees.

Algae creates a genuinely dangerous slip hazard. On smooth surfaces like tarmac or sealed block paving, wet algae has roughly the same friction coefficient as ice. The Royal Society for the Prevention of Accidents estimates that outdoor slips on biological growth account for thousands of A&E visits annually.

**The DIY problem:** Consumer pressure washers remove surface algae but leave the root system intact. Within 4–6 weeks, the green returns. Professional operators use biocide pre-treatment that kills algae at the root, preventing regrowth for 12–18 months.

**When to act:** If the green is visible when the surface is dry, it's well established and needs professional treatment. Don't wait for it to become slippery.

## 2. Weeds Are Growing Through Block Paving Joints

Block paving is extremely common across Surrey — Same Day Jet Wash, a pressure washing service operating across Redhill, Reigate, and the wider Surrey area, reports that weed-infested block paving is their single most common callout.

Here's why weeds keep coming back when you pull them out manually:

| Method | Weed Removal | Root Removal | Regrowth Time | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hand pulling | Surface only | Partial | 2–4 weeks | Free (but hours of work) |
| Domestic weedkiller | Surface kill | Partial root kill | 4–8 weeks | £5–£15 |
| Consumer pressure washer | Good | Partial | 6–12 weeks | £40–£80 hire |
| Professional clean + re-sand | Complete | Complete | 12–24 months | £150–£350 |

The critical difference is **re-sanding**. After professional cleaning, joints are filled with fresh kiln-dried sand that physically blocks new seed germination. This single step is what makes professional results last 10x longer than DIY.

**When to act:** If you're pulling weeds from your driveway more than once a month, the jointing sand is depleted and needs professional restoration.

## 3. Oil Stains Are Spreading

Every car drips small amounts of oil, and over time these accumulate into dark patches. On block paving and concrete, oil soaks into the porous surface and becomes progressively harder to remove.

**The chemistry matters:** Oil creates a chemical bond with porous materials that water pressure alone cannot break. Professional equipment uses heated water at 3,000+ PSI combined with specialist degreasing agents that break down the oil molecule before it's blasted away.

**Time is critical:**
- Fresh stains (under 1 week): 95%+ removable
- Recent stains (1–3 months): 80–90% removable
- Old stains (6+ months): 50–70% removable — significant lightening but ghost marks may remain
- Ancient stains (1+ years): Lightening only — the oil has permanently stained the substrate

For a detailed treatment guide, Same Day Jet Wash has published an [oil stain removal guide](https://samedayjetwash.com/guides/remove-oil-stains-driveway) covering different surface types and treatment methods.

**When to act:** The sooner the better. If you can see a dark patch where you park, don't wait.

## 4. You Can't Remember What Colour Your Driveway Used To Be

This is the most common sign, and it happens so gradually that homeowners don't notice until they see a neighbour's freshly cleaned driveway for comparison.

Block paving that was once vibrant red or charcoal fades to a uniform grey-brown. Concrete darkens. Tarmac loses its rich black colour. The original colour is almost always still there — buried under layers of dirt, atmospheric pollution, algae, and traffic film.

The transformation from professional pressure washing is dramatic. The Homeowners Alliance reports that exterior cleaning is one of the highest-ROI home improvements — for £150–£300, you get a visual transformation equivalent to spending thousands on landscaping.

**The before/after test:** Pressure wash a small test patch (30cm × 30cm) with a garden hose on full power. If there's a noticeable colour difference between the washed and unwashed areas, imagine that difference across your entire driveway.

**When to act:** If a neighbour's freshly cleaned driveway makes yours look tired, or if visitors comment on it, the accumulated grime is affecting your property's appearance.

## 5. You're About to Sell, Rent, or Remortgage

First impressions are formed in 7 seconds. For property viewings, that means the approach from the street — your driveway, front path, and entrance — sets the tone before a buyer steps through the door.

According to the National Association of Estate Agents, kerb appeal is consistently rated as one of the top 5 factors influencing buyer decisions. A clean, well-maintained exterior signals that the property has been cared for — the opposite assumption (poor maintenance = hidden problems) can knock thousands off offers.

For landlords, a clean exterior helps attract quality tenants and justifies market-rate rents. For homeowners remortgaging, the surveyor's valuation is partly based on property condition and presentation.

**When to act:** Book a professional clean 1–2 weeks before your property goes on the market or before viewings begin.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I know if my driveway needs professional cleaning or just a hose down?**
If a garden hose on full pressure doesn't remove the discolouration, it's beyond DIY territory. Green growth visible on dry surfaces, weeds in paving joints, and oil stains all require professional equipment and treatment to resolve properly.

**How long does professional driveway cleaning take?**
A standard single driveway (25m²) takes 2–3 hours including setup, pre-treatment, cleaning, and re-sanding. Larger areas or heavily soiled surfaces take 4–6 hours. You don't need to be home during the work.

**Will pressure washing damage my driveway?**
Not when done by a professional who adjusts pressure for your specific surface. The risk comes from DIY use at incorrect PSI settings — particularly on natural stone, pointing, and timber. Professional operators use 2,000 PSI for delicate surfaces and 3,500+ PSI for robust materials like concrete.

**How often should I have my driveway professionally cleaned?**
Every 12–18 months in the UK climate. Shaded driveways under trees may need annual cleaning. Adding sealant after cleaning extends the cycle to 3–5 years for block paving.

**Is professional cleaning worth the cost?**
For most UK homeowners, yes. The cost (£80–£300 for a typical driveway) delivers results lasting 12–18 months, compared to 3–6 months from consumer hire equipment. When you factor in 4–6 hours of your weekend for DIY, the professional option is often better value.
