If you have ever asked a pressure washing company "how much to clean my driveway?" and got the answer "it depends", this is the piece that explains what it actually depends on, and roughly what you should expect to pay in Surrey in 2026.

The short version: professional exterior cleaning in Surrey runs about £3 to £8 per square metre for most surfaces, with a minimum charge of around £80. But the per-metre rate is only half the story. Two driveways of exactly the same size can be quoted hundreds of pounds apart, and once you understand why, you can judge whether a quote is fair.

## First, the five things that move the price

Before the surface-by-surface numbers, these are the factors that decide where in the range you land:

1. **Size.** Larger areas cost less per square metre, because the setup time (unloading kit, running hoses, mixing treatments) is spread across more surface. A 50m² driveway is not double the price of a 25m² one.
2. **Condition.** A driveway cleaned last year costs less than one that has been left for five. Heavy moss, thick black spot or embedded oil all add treatment time.
3. **Surface type.** Robust concrete and tarmac clean quickly at high pressure. Natural stone and render need slower, gentler, low-pressure techniques, which costs more per metre.
4. **Access and extras.** Easy ground-level access keeps costs down. Extras like re-sanding block paving joints, sealing, or treating oil stains add to the base price.
5. **Location.** South East prices sit roughly 10 to 15 per cent above the UK average, reflecting higher operating costs in the region.

## What each surface costs

Here are typical 2026 Surrey figures. Full detail for our own rates is on the [pricing page](https://samedayjetwash.com/pricing).

**Driveways.** Concrete and tarmac come in around £3 to £5 per square metre. Block paving is £4 to £6 for a clean, or £5 to £9 if you include re-sanding the joints (which you usually should, more on that below). In cash terms a small single driveway is roughly £80 to £150, a large double £150 to £300. Because block paving is so common across Redhill, Reigate, Horley and Crawley estates, it is the surface most people are asking about, and there is a dedicated [driveway cleaning](https://samedayjetwash.com/driveway-cleaning) page with the specifics.

**Patios.** Concrete slabs and porcelain are £3 to £5 per square metre. Natural stone, and Indian sandstone in particular, is £6 to £10 because it needs specialist low-pressure soft washing and often a black spot (lichen) treatment that standard cleaning will not shift. A small patio lands around £60 to £150, a large one £240 to £400.

**Render.** Rendered walls are cleaned by soft washing, a low-pressure biocide treatment rather than a high-pressure jet, which would damage the surface. Budget £5 to £10 per square metre, so a typical two-storey house front is £200 to £500.

**Decking.** Around £6 to £8 per square metre, so a normal garden deck is £80 to £200. Worth doing yearly, since decking gets genuinely slippery with winter algae.

## The block paving detail worth knowing

If your driveway is block paving, the single most important line item is whether re-sanding is included. After cleaning, fresh kiln-dried sand should be brushed back into the joints. That sand physically blocks new weed seeds and keeps the blocks stable. Skip it and the weeds are back within weeks. A quote that includes re-sanding is often better value than a cheaper one that leaves the joints empty, even though the headline number is higher. When you compare quotes, ask the question directly.

## Is professional worth it over DIY hire?

Hiring a domestic machine looks cheaper until you count your time and the risk. Professional kit runs at 3,000 to 4,000 PSI with a much higher flow rate and a surface-cleaner attachment that avoids the zebra-stripe finish DIY lances leave. In practice a standard driveway is 2 to 3 hours for a pro versus 5 to 6 hours with hire kit, and professional results last 12 to 18 months against 3 to 6 for DIY. The bigger risk is damage: too much pressure on sandstone, pointing or timber causes harm that costs more to put right than the clean would have.
