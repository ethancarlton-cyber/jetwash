Why pressure-washing a tile roof shortens its life by years

Concrete and clay tiles have a protective surface layer put on at the factory. Blast that with a high-pressure jet and you strip it off in one pass. The tiles look clean for a season, then they start drinking up water, cracking in the frost and ageing faster than the warranty ever planned for. Pressure also drives water up under the tile laps and into your loft, which you really don’t want. The only right way is soft wash: scrape the moss off by hand, kill the rest with a biocide (Lithofin Algex[1] or similar), and let the weather finish it. Most Surrey roofs are good for five to ten years between cleans, sooner if you’re under trees on the shaded side.

Why pressure washing a roof is a bad idea

Concrete and clay tiles have a protective surface layer applied at the factory. High-pressure water strips that off in one pass, the tiles look clean for a season, then start absorbing water and degrading much faster. Pressure also forces water up under the tile laps and into the loft. The Marshalls protocol[2] developed for hard paving is well above the maximum pressure any tile roof should ever see. Soft wash is the only correct method for roofs. Scrape the moss, kill the rest with biocide, let nature finish the job.

Common mistakes with roof cleaning

Roof cleaning is higher-ticket than a driveway, so it pulls in more doorstep operators who do it wrong. The usual ones:

  • Pressure washing the tiles. The big one. It strips the factory coating, can crack older clay tiles and forces water under the laps into the loft. NFRC and the tile manufacturers advise against it, and one afternoon of blasting can void the tile warranty.
  • No biocide afterwards. Scrape or blast the moss off and walk away and it’s back within 6–12 months, because the spores are still in the surface. The biocide is what buys you 3–5 years.
  • Walking a wet roof. A mossy or freshly-treated roof is a slip hazard, and foot traffic cracks tiles. Proper access (ladders, towers, harness, a cherry picker where the pitch needs it) is the job, not an optional extra.
  • Ignoring the gutters. All that scraped moss has to go somewhere. Leave it and the gutters block, water backs up, and the mossy debris drops onto the paving below and drags its slip resistance down.

The warranty angle, the real reason to soft-wash

This is the part the cheap doorstep operators won’t tell you. Major tile manufacturers and roofing bodies, GAF, ARMA, and UK NFRC guidance, advise against high-pressure washing of roof tiles. Blast a tile roof and you can void the tile manufacturer’s warranty in one afternoon. Hand removal plus soft wash keeps the factory coating intact, keeps your warranty valid, and keeps the job defensible. We’d rather do it the slow, safe way than save an hour and cost you a re-roof.

How long a roof clean lasts, the regrowth timeline

The short answer: 3–5 years with a proper biocide treatment, versus only 6–12 months if a roof is just pressure-washed and left untreated. The difference is the residual surface inhibitor: the biocide kills the moss, lichen and algae spores at root level and leaves a film on the tile that suppresses regrowth through the following seasons. A wash-only clean strips the green off but leaves the spores, so it comes straight back. North-facing pitches, tree-lined plots and heavily shaded roofs regrow faster, on those we’d suggest a lighter biocide top-up between full cleans rather than waiting for the moss to take hold again. Worth keeping two numbers apart: the biocide’s protective film lasts 3–5 years (top-up territory), while a full re-clean only comes round every 5–10 years on a sun-exposed roof, sooner on shaded or tree-covered ones, as our cadence matrix above shows.

Should you clean your roof before selling?

Short answer: yes. A streaked, mossy roof makes a house look older and tired in listing photos, and a heavily mossed roof can flag on a buyer’s survey, handing them a negotiating point to knock money off. A clean roof lifts kerb appeal and removes that red flag. Best done a few weeks before your listing photos so the biocide has started clearing the last of the green. We walk through the timing and what surveyors actually look for in our guide on roof cleaning before selling a house.

Best time to book

Late winter to early spring is the ideal window. Treating the roof then gives the biocide the whole growing season to work through the moss, lichen and algae, you get the cleanest result by summer. That said, a roof clean works at any time of year; the timing just affects how quickly you see the full result. If you’re unsure how overdue yours is, our guide on how often you should clean your roof covers the signs and the right cadence for your tile type and aspect.

Will cleaning damage my tiles?

Not with the right method, and this is the question most Surrey homeowners worry about most. Hand removal and soft wash protect the tiles; it’s high pressure (above roughly 1,200 psi) that strips coatings, cracks older clay tiles and forces water under the laps. Substrate-driven roof cadence by postcode

Roof moss cadence varies materially by postcode and tree shade. Chalk-substrate areas (SM7 Banstead, parts of RH2, CR3 Caterham, CR5 Chipstead) sit on BGS Chalk Group[5] with excellent drainage but heavy tree canopy in Kingswood Warren, Banstead Woods and the Caterham Valley. Roof pitches under that canopy match the “shaded” column of our matrix even when the rest of the property is on well-drained substrate. Weald Clay areas (RH6 Horley, RH10/RH11 Crawley) on BGS Weald Clay[6] with flatter terrain and higher humidity push roof cadence to the shorter end of the range.

Slip-risk and PTV from the ground perspective

Roof moss isn’t a slip-risk on the roof itself, nobody walks on a tile roof except during a job. But moss falling onto paving below is. UKSRG / HSE pendulum guidance[3] at PTV ≥36 applies on the paving. A heavily mossed roof drops mossy debris into the gutters and onto path/patio surfaces, dragging down adjacent paving PTV. Gutter clearance as part of the roof clean closes that loop.

Roof cleaning FAQs

Most-asked questions from Surrey customers.

Will cleaning damage my roof tiles?

Not with the right method. We hand-remove moss and soft-wash rather than high-pressure jetting, which protects the tiles’ factory coating and your manufacturer warranty. Pressure over roughly 1,200 psi can crack tiles and force water underneath. Full honest answer here.

How long does roof cleaning last?

With biocide treatment, typically 3–5 years before moss returns, versus only 6–12 months if a roof is just pressure-washed without treatment. North-facing, shaded or tree-lined roofs regrow faster.

How much does roof cleaning cost in Surrey?

Most Surrey homes fall between roughly £350 and £900, rising to around £1,200 for heavy moss or a full biocide treatment, depending on size, pitch, access and moss severity. South East prices run about 20–30% above the UK average. We give a written, itemised quote, no doorstep pressure.

Is moss actually bad for my roof?

It can be. Moss holds water against the tiles, and freeze-thaw cycles can lift or crack them; debris also blocks gutters and valleys. Removing it carefully with the right method extends tile life.

Do you pressure wash or soft wash?

We hand-remove moss and soft-wash with biocide. We avoid high-pressure jetting on tiles because it strips protective coatings, can void warranties and rarely lasts. Steam is available for delicate or heritage roofs.

How often should a roof be cleaned?

There are two different intervals here, so it’s worth separating them. A full re-clean (hand-scrape plus biocide) only needs doing every 5 to 10 years on most sun-exposed Surrey roofs, sooner on shaded, north-facing or tree-covered pitches. In between, a light biocide top-up every 2 to 3 years keeps regrowth at bay so you don’t reach a full clean again as quickly. Shaded or heavily wooded properties want the shorter end of both. See our full cadence guide.

Do you need scaffolding?

Most domestic cleans are done from ladders and access towers; scaffolding or a cherry picker is only needed for steep or awkward roofs and is quoted separately. We work to Work at Height safety standards.

Should I clean my roof before selling?

Yes, a streaked, mossy roof can flag on a survey and make a house look older. A clean roof improves kerb appeal and removes a negotiating point for buyers. Best done a few weeks before listing photos. More on selling here.

How long does the job take?

A semi-detached roof is usually 3–5 hours; a larger detached can be 6–7 hours with straightforward access. The biocide then continues working over the following weeks.

Can you clean all types of roofs, and do you cover my town?

Yes, concrete tile, clay tile, natural slate, synthetic slate, and flat roofs (felt, EPDM, fibreglass). We cover Redhill, Reigate, Crawley, Horley, Dorking, Banstead and across RH1–RH11, CR3, SM7 and KT17–KT24 within roughly 20 miles of Redhill.
