Restore Your Roof with Professional Soft Washing

Over time, roofs across Surrey accumulate thick layers of moss, lichen, and algae that do more than just look unsightly. Left untreated, biological growth traps moisture against your tiles, accelerates deterioration, and can lead to costly repairs. I provide professional roof cleaning in Redhill, Reigate and across Surrey that safely removes all biological growth, restoring your roof's appearance and extending its lifespan by years.

Unlike amateur approaches that rely on high-pressure washing, I use specialist soft wash techniques specifically designed for roof cleaning. This means your tiles, slates, or flat roof membrane are cleaned thoroughly without the risk of damage that comes with aggressive pressure washing. The result is a cleaner roof that stays cleaner for longer.

Why Not DIY?

Never pressure wash a roof yourself. The height risk alone isn't worth it, and incorrect pressure strips granules from tiles, shortening their lifespan. I use safe low-pressure methods from proper access equipment.

Why Roof Cleaning Matters

Many homeowners overlook their roof until problems become visible from ground level. By that point, moss growth is usually well established and may already be causing damage. Here's why regular roof cleaning is essential for Surrey properties:

  • Prevents Tile Damage - Moss roots penetrate tile surfaces, causing cracks and lifting that leads to leaks and expensive repairs
  • Improves Property Value - A clean roof dramatically enhances kerb appeal and adds value when selling your home
  • Extends Roof Lifespan - Regular cleaning can add 10-15 years to your roof's serviceable life
  • Reduces Energy Costs - Heavy moss growth acts as insulation in summer but traps dampness in winter, reducing your roof's thermal efficiency
  • Prevents Water Damage - Moss and debris block gutters and valleys, causing water to back up under tiles and into your property
  • Maintains Insurance Cover - Some insurers may refuse claims for water damage if roof maintenance has been neglected

Our Roof Cleaning Process

1. Roof Survey & Assessment

We inspect your roof from ground level and, where safe, from ladder height to assess the type and extent of growth. We check tile condition, identify any existing damage, and plan the safest approach for your specific roof.

2. Manual Moss Removal

Heavy moss growth is carefully scraped and brushed from tiles by hand. This removes the bulk of the moss without dislodging tiles or forcing debris into gaps. All removed moss is collected and disposed of.

3. Soft Wash Treatment

We apply a professional-grade biocide solution across the entire roof surface using low-pressure equipment. This treatment kills remaining moss, lichen, and algae spores at the root level, preventing rapid regrowth.

4. Gutter Clearance & Final Check

All gutters and downpipes are cleared of debris from the cleaning process. We conduct a final inspection to ensure complete coverage and check for any tile issues that need attention.

Roof Types We Clean in Surrey

Concrete Tile Roofs

The most common roof type across Surrey, concrete tiles are particularly prone to moss growth. Our soft wash method safely removes years of accumulated growth, restoring the tile colour without damaging the surface. Concrete tiles in areas like Horley and Crawley are especially susceptible due to proximity to wooded areas.

Clay Tile Roofs

Clay tiles are more delicate than concrete and should never be pressure washed. Our soft wash approach is ideal for clay roofs, gently removing lichen and moss while preserving the tile's natural patina. Many period properties in Reigate and Dorking have original clay tile roofs that benefit from careful cleaning.

Natural Slate Roofs

Slate roofs require particular care as individual slates can crack or dislodge if handled incorrectly. We use the gentlest possible approach for slate cleaning, relying primarily on biocide treatment rather than mechanical removal. This preserves the integrity of each slate while effectively killing biological growth.

Flat Roofs

Flat roofs collect standing water and organic debris, making them prone to algae and moss growth. We clean flat roof membranes using low-pressure methods that won't compromise waterproof seals or cause ponding issues.

Common Roof Problems in Surrey

Surrey's climate creates ideal conditions for biological growth on roofs. Our customers across Redhill, Banstead, and the wider area commonly report these issues:

  • Thick Moss Growth - Surrey's damp, mild climate and tree-lined streets encourage rapid moss colonisation, particularly on north-facing roof slopes
  • Lichen Patches - Lichen grows slowly but bonds strongly to tile surfaces, creating grey-green patches that are almost impossible to remove without professional treatment
  • Green Algae Staining - Dark green or black algae streaks are common on roofs near trees, spreading gradually across the entire roof surface
  • Blocked Valleys & Gutters - Moss and debris accumulate in roof valleys and gutters, causing water overflow and potential damp problems inside your property
  • Lifted or Cracked Tiles - Moss roots grow between and under tiles, gradually lifting them and creating entry points for rainwater
  • Ridge Tile Deterioration - Mortar between ridge tiles crumbles faster when moss holds moisture against it, leading to loose ridge tiles

Soft Washing vs Pressure Washing for Roofs

We strongly recommend soft washing over pressure washing for all roof types. Here's why:

Pressure washing risks: High-pressure water can crack tiles, strip protective coatings, force water under tiles causing interior damp, dislodge ridge tiles, and damage flashing. It removes moss temporarily but doesn't kill spores, meaning regrowth happens within months.

Soft washing benefits: Low-pressure application is safe for all tile types. Professional biocide kills moss, lichen, and algae at the root level. Results last 3-5 years compared to 6-12 months with pressure washing alone. No risk of tile or membrane damage.

How much does roof cleaning cost in Surrey?

Roof cleaning in Surrey typically costs between £15-30 per square metre, with most residential roofs costing £800-2,000 in total. The exact price depends on roof size, pitch, tile type, and the extent of moss or lichen growth. We provide free, no-obligation quotes with our 2-hour callback guarantee. Is pressure washing safe for roof tiles?

High-pressure washing can damage roof tiles by stripping protective coatings and forcing water under tiles. That's why we use soft washing for most roof cleaning jobs. Soft washing uses low-pressure water combined with specialist biocide treatments to safely kill and remove moss, lichen, and algae without risking tile damage.

How long does roof cleaning take?

A typical residential roof clean takes 1-2 days depending on the roof size, pitch, and condition. The initial clean removes bulk moss and debris, followed by biocide treatment. Some treatments continue working for several weeks after application, gradually killing remaining growth.

How often should I have my roof cleaned?

We recommend professional roof cleaning every 3-5 years in Surrey. Properties surrounded by trees or in shaded positions may need cleaning more frequently due to faster moss and lichen growth. Regular cleaning prevents damage to tiles and extends your roof's lifespan significantly.

Can you clean all types of roofs?

Yes, we clean all common roof types including concrete tiles, clay tiles, natural slate, synthetic slate, and flat roofs. Each material requires a specific approach - we adjust our cleaning method, pressure settings, and chemical treatments to suit your particular roof type for safe, effective results.
