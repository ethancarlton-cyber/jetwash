How often should you clean your driveway?

Every 1–2 years for most UK drives. Annually if you’re shaded, under trees, or in a damp microclimate. Here’s how to work out the right schedule for yours.

7 min read · Updated February 2026

Key facts
  • Most UK drives: every 1–2 years
  • Block paving: 12–18 months due to joint moss
  • Resin bound: 24–36 months — smooth surface stays clean
  • Sealed drives: stretch to 3–4 years between cleans
  • Spring is the natural slot — book early, slots fill fast

The quick answer: most UK drives need a proper professional clean every 1–2 years. Block paving sits at the shorter end (12–18 months) because moss takes hold in the joints. Resin bound stretches to 2–3 years. Sealed drives go even longer. The right schedule depends on shade, trees, traffic, and the surface material.

This guide walks through how to spot the signs, how the surface affects the schedule, and the cost picture if you let it slide too long.

Signs your drive needs cleaning

  • Green or black patches — algae or moss is taking hold
  • Slippery when wet — biofilm. Genuine slip hazard
  • Weeds in the joints — the joint sand has gone
  • White salt deposits — efflorescence, common on newer block paving
  • Spreading oil stains — deal with these fast or they set in permanently
  • Faded, dull look — surface dirt has dulled the original colour

What changes the schedule

Shade & tree cover

North-facing or tree-shaded drives need cleaning every 12 months. Sunny south-facing can stretch to 2–3 years. Tree leaves drop in autumn and the tannins stain — that’s an extra clean cycle in itself.

Climate

Surrey is mild and wet enough that block paving picks up moss in 12–18 months. Wetter regions (west country) accelerate the cycle. Drier east of England can stretch the interval.

Traffic & oil

Heavy traffic and frequent oil drips shorten the cycle. Drives with two or three vehicles parking daily collect more dirt than single-vehicle drives.

Sealing status

Sealed drives stay cleaner for 3–4 years. The sealer blocks weed germination, shrugs off oil, and keeps the colour fresh.

DIY frequency vs professional

DIY light maintenance can be done more often (every 6–12 months) but proper deep cleaning with hot water, biocide, and re-sanding only needs to happen every 1–2 years professionally. Hot water kills moss roots; cold-water DIY only takes off the visible layer.

What happens if you never clean it?

  • Permanent staining — oil and tannins set in
  • Block paving lifts — moss roots prise blocks apart
  • Slip hazard — algae becomes dangerous in wet weather
  • Weed infestation — joints break down, water gets in, sub-base damaged
  • Property value — estate agents flag dirty drives as a kerb-appeal hit
  • Resurfacing cost — severe neglect means £2,000–£10,000 to replace, not £200 to clean

Best time of year

Spring (March–May) is the peak cleaning season. Winter moss has peaked but not hardened — easier to remove. Late winter and early spring are typically 10–15% cheaper before the rush. Autumn cleans (September–October) leave you with safe surfaces through winter. Avoid hard frosts (water freezes) and peak summer (premium pricing).

Cleaning frequency FAQs

The questions Surrey customers ask most often.

How often should I clean my drive?

Every 1–2 years for most UK drives. Shaded or wet-climate drives may need annual cleaning; sunny well-drained drives stretch to 2–3 years.

What are the signs?

Green or black discolouration, slippery surfaces when wet, weeds in joints, white salt deposits, oil stains spreading, faded look. If it looks noticeably different to when it was new, it’s time.

Does the surface affect frequency?

Yes. Block paving 12–18 months due to moss in joints. Resin bound 2–3 years. Tarmac shows dirt quickly but cleans easily. Sandstone porous, more often than granite.

Is annual cleaning worth it?

Not necessary for most, but worthwhile if you’re heavily shaded, in a wet area, or have moss-prone block paving. Maintains property value and prevents permanent damage.

DIY or professional?

DIY for light maintenance, professional for deep cleans every 1–2 years. Pros use 3,000+ PSI machines, hot water (kills moss roots), and rotary cleaners for even results.

What if I never clean it?

Permanent staining, lifted block paving, slip hazards, weed infestation, joint damage, lower property value. Severe neglect means resurfacing (£2,000+) instead of cleaning (£200).

Does cleaning affect property value?

Yes. Estate agents say kerb appeal can affect property value by 5–10%. £150–£200 clean is excellent ROI when selling.

Before or after winter?

Both work. Before-winter clean removes slippery algae. After-winter removes grime and salt. Spring is most popular; autumn gives safer surfaces through winter.
