What's Included in Block Paving Cleaning?

Block paving cleaning involves more steps than cleaning other driveway surfaces. The joints between blocks create additional work that is essential for a good result.

Standard Block Paving Clean Includes:

  • Weed removal: Pulling or scraping weeds from joints before pressure washing. Deep-rooted weeds need clearing first for the cleaning to be effective.
  • Joint sand removal: Old, contaminated sand is washed out of joints during the cleaning process. This is unavoidable and is why re-sanding is essential.
  • Pre-treatment: Application of moss and algae killer to loosen biological growth before washing. Our moss removal guide explains why this step is essential.
  • High-pressure washing: Professional-grade pressure washing using rotary surface cleaners for even, streak-free results across all blocks.
  • Edge and detail work: Cleaning around borders, kerbs, drain covers, and expansion joints that rotary cleaners cannot reach.
  • Re-sanding: Once the surface has dried, fresh kiln-dried sand is swept into every joint. This stabilises the blocks, prevents weed regrowth, and deters ants from nesting.
  • Debris removal: All waste material, old sand, and moss cleaned up and removed from site.

Optional Extras:

  • Sealing: £3-8/sqm additional. Applied after cleaning and re-sanding. Protects against staining, weed growth, colour fading, and oil penetration. Lasts 3-5 years.
  • Oil stain treatment: £10-30 per stain. Old oil stains need specialist degreaser application before or during cleaning. Our guide on removing oil stains from driveways covers the treatment process.
  • Efflorescence treatment: £30-60. White salt deposits on the surface, common on newer block paving, require acid-based treatment to remove.
  • Ant treatment: £20-40. Ant nests in the sub-base can undermine blocks. Insecticide treatment prevents recurrence after cleaning.
  • Individual block replacement: £5-15 per block. Cracked, chipped, or sunken blocks can be replaced during the cleaning visit.
  • Re-levelling sunken areas: £15-30 per sqm. Where blocks have sunk due to sub-base settlement, they can be lifted, re-compacted, and relaid.

  • Best for: Budget-conscious homeowners, driveways cleaned regularly, areas with minimal shade

Cleaning and Sealing

  • Cost: £6.50-14/sqm
  • Results last: 3-5 years with dramatically reduced weed growth
  • Maintenance: Occasional sweeping only — weeds struggle to establish in sealed joints
  • Best for: Long-term value, driveways with persistent weed problems, colour enhancement, oil stain prevention

What Sealing Actually Does

Block paving sealer is a liquid coating applied after cleaning. It penetrates the surface of the blocks and fills the pores, creating a barrier against moisture, UV light, and organic growth. The result is a surface that resists staining, maintains its colour, and stays cleaner for far longer.

Sealer also locks the kiln-dried sand into the joints, dramatically reducing weed germination. Most customers who add sealing report 80-90% less weed growth compared to unsealed paving.

There are two main types of block paving sealer:

  • Matt/natural finish: £3-5/sqm. Preserves the natural look of the blocks while providing invisible protection. Recommended for traditional or rustic-style block paving.
  • Wet look/gloss finish: £4-8/sqm. Enhances the colour of the blocks and gives a "wet" appearance. Popular for darker or multi-coloured block paving where colour vibrancy is desired.

Factors That Affect Block Paving Cleaning Cost

1. Condition of the Paving

Block paving that was cleaned within the last 18 months takes less time and costs less than paving that hasn't been touched in 5+ years. Heavy moss in joints, thick weed growth, and embedded dirt all add time to the job. Our block paving cleaning service handles even the most neglected driveways. Expect a 20-30% premium for heavily neglected paving.

2. Age of the Block Paving

Newer block paving (under 5 years) may have efflorescence (white salt deposits) that requires additional treatment. Very old block paving (20+ years) may have faded colour that responds beautifully to cleaning and sealing but might have blocks that need replacing.

3. Complexity of the Layout

Simple rectangular driveways are fastest to clean. Curved borders, intricate herringbone patterns, decorative bands, and multiple levels all add time because edges and transitions need hand-finishing. Complex layouts can add 10-20% to the price.

4. Access and Water Supply

Easy access keeps costs standard. If equipment needs to be transported through the house or up steep slopes, expect a £20-50 surcharge. Properties without an outside tap may need a water tank, adding further setup time.

5. Location

Surrey prices (£3.50-6/sqm) are at the upper end of the UK range. Within Surrey, prices are fairly consistent, with slightly lower rates in more competitive areas like Crawley versus premium postcodes like Reigate Hill or Cobham.

6. Combined Services

Having your block paving driveway cleaned alongside your patio, paths, or walls saves 10-20% compared to separate bookings. Most companies offer better per-sqm rates for combined jobs over 60sqm.

The Block Paving Cleaning Process

Understanding the professional cleaning process helps you know what to expect and evaluate whether a company is doing a thorough job.

Step 1: Assessment and Setup (15-30 minutes)

The cleaner assesses the paving condition, identifies any problem areas (oil stains, sunken blocks, heavy moss), and sets up equipment. Water supply is connected and the pressure washer is configured for the appropriate settings.

Step 2: Weed and Moss Removal (30-60 minutes)

Large weeds are pulled or scraped from joints. Heavy moss growth may be scraped off manually before washing. For badly neglected driveways, a biocide pre-treatment is applied and left to work for 15-30 minutes.

Step 3: Pressure Washing (1-3 hours depending on size)

Using a rotary surface cleaner at 2000-2500 PSI, the entire surface is systematically cleaned in overlapping passes. Edges, borders, and detail areas are finished with a hand lance at reduced pressure. Old joint sand is washed out during this process.

Step 4: Rinse and Inspect (15-30 minutes)

The entire area is rinsed down and inspected for any missed areas, stubborn stains, or damage. Any remaining problem areas are addressed.

Step 5: Drying (4-24 hours)

The paving needs to dry before re-sanding. In warm, breezy weather this takes 4-6 hours. In cool or humid conditions, it may take 24 hours. Some cleaners return the next day for re-sanding.

Step 6: Re-Sanding (30-60 minutes)

Kiln-dried sand is poured onto the dry surface and swept into every joint using a stiff broom. Excess sand is swept off. A vibrating plate compactor is used if available to settle the sand firmly into the joints.

Step 7: Sealing (if requested, 1-2 hours)

Sealer is applied in 2 coats using a roller or low-pressure sprayer. Each coat needs 2-4 hours to dry. The driveway should not be walked on or driven over for 24 hours after sealing.

Why Re-Sanding Is Essential

Re-sanding after cleaning is not optional — it's a critical maintenance step that many DIY cleaners skip, leading to rapid deterioration of the paving.

Kiln-dried sand in the joints serves three essential functions:

  • Structural stability: The sand locks blocks together, distributing weight evenly. Without sand, blocks shift under vehicle weight, creating uneven surfaces and gaps.
  • Weed prevention: Tightly packed sand leaves no space for weed seeds to germinate. Loose or missing sand allows weeds to establish within weeks.
  • Insect deterrent: Ants are attracted to the warm, dry voids in empty block paving joints. Their tunnelling undermines the sub-base, causing blocks to sink. Properly sanded joints discourage nesting.

A 25kg bag of kiln-dried sand costs £4-6 from any DIY store and covers approximately 15-20sqm. For a full driveway re-sand, you'll need 2-4 bags depending on the area and joint width.

Best Time to Clean Block Paving

Timing affects both cost and results. Spring (March–May) is the ideal season for block paving cleaning in the UK — winter moss and algae have peaked, but haven't hardened, making removal easier and faster. Professional cleaners are typically 10–15% cheaper in late winter and early spring before the peak season rush.

How Long Block Paving Cleaning Lasts

A professional clean with re-sanding typically keeps block paving looking good for 2–3 years. Adding sealing extends this to 4–5 years by creating a protective barrier against moss, algae, and weed growth. The longevity depends on three factors: how much shade the area receives (shaded driveways deteriorate faster), local tree cover (leaf debris accelerates staining), and whether the paving is sealed after cleaning.

For most Surrey homeowners, a cleaning cycle of every 2–3 years is the most cost-effective approach. Annual cleaning is unnecessary unless the paving is heavily shaded or under trees. Frequently Asked Questions

How much does block paving cleaning cost?

Block paving cleaning costs £3.50-6 per sqm in 2026, including re-sanding. A typical 40sqm driveway costs £140-240 for cleaning, or £260-560 with sealing added.

Is re-sanding included in the price?

With reputable companies, yes. Re-sanding with kiln-dried sand should be included as standard. If a quote doesn't mention re-sanding, ask for it to be added — it's essential for the longevity of your block paving.

How often should block paving be cleaned?

Every 12-18 months for unsealed paving. Sealed block paving can go 2-3 years between professional cleans. Regular sweeping and spot weed treatment extends the time between visits.

Is sealing worth the extra cost?

For most driveways, yes. Sealing adds £3-8/sqm but prevents 80-90% of weed growth, protects colour, resists oil stains, and means the paving stays cleaner 2-3x longer. Over 3-5 years, sealing typically pays for itself in reduced maintenance.
