Pressure Washer Hire vs Professional

An honest cost comparison to help you decide whether hiring a pressure washer or calling professionals delivers better value.

The honest answer: Hiring a pressure washer is cheaper in upfront cash costs, but when you factor in time, consumables, and the shorter duration of results, professional cleaning often delivers better value. The exception is small, lightly-soiled areas where DIY genuinely makes sense.

We're a professional pressure washing company, so you might expect bias. But we believe in honest advice - sometimes hiring makes sense, sometimes it doesn't. This guide gives you the real numbers to decide for yourself.

Cost Breakdown: DIY Pressure Washer Hire

Let's look at the true costs of hiring a pressure washer for a typical 40 square metre driveway:

Visible Hire Costs

  • Pressure washer hire (decent 2000+ PSI): £40-80 per day
  • Patio/surface cleaner attachment: £15-25 extra (if not included)
  • Collection/delivery: £0 if you collect, £15-30 if delivered

Hidden Costs (Often Forgotten)

  • Cleaning chemicals: £10-25
  • Kiln-dried sand for re-sanding: £15-30 (essential for block paving)
  • Petrol (petrol machines): £10-20
  • Extension lead/hose: £15-40 if you don't own suitable ones
  • Safety goggles: £5-10 (essential)
  • Waterproof clothing: £20-50 (you will get wet)
  • Damage deposit: £50-200 (refundable if no damage)

Time Cost (The Big One)

  • Collection/return: 1-2 hours
  • Setup and learning: 30-60 minutes
  • Actual cleaning (40sqm): 3-4 hours
  • Re-sanding (block paving): 1 hour
  • Cleanup: 30 minutes
  • Total time: 6-8 hours

Total DIY Cost

For a 40sqm driveway:

  • Cash costs: £80-165
  • Time investment: 6-8 hours
  • If you value time at £15/hour: £90-120 additional value
  • True total cost: £170-285

Types of Pressure Washers Available for Hire

Understanding what's available helps you make informed decisions:

Electric Pressure Washers (1400-2000 PSI)

  • Hire cost: £30-50 per day
  • Best for: Light cleaning, cars, garden furniture
  • Driveway suitability: Struggles with established dirt/moss
  • Considerations: Limited reach (needs extension lead), quieter operation

Mid-Range Pressure Washers (2000-2500 PSI)

  • Hire cost: £50-70 per day
  • Best for: General driveway cleaning, patios
  • Driveway suitability: Adequate for most residential jobs
  • Considerations: Sweet spot for DIY - capable but manageable

Professional-Grade (2500-3500 PSI)

  • Hire cost: £70-100 per day
  • Best for: Heavy soiling, commercial work
  • Driveway suitability: Very effective but requires care
  • Considerations: Higher damage risk if misused, may require training

Petrol vs Electric

  • Electric: Quieter, lighter, no fuel cost, but limited by cord length and typically lower pressure
  • Petrol: More powerful, fully portable, but heavier, noisier, and needs fuel. Better for large areas

Skill Requirements and Learning Curve

Pressure washing looks simple but has a real learning curve:

What You Need to Learn

  • Correct distance: Too close damages surfaces, too far is ineffective
  • Consistent technique: Uneven passes create visible stripes
  • Nozzle selection: Wrong nozzle causes damage or poor results
  • Pressure adjustment: Different surfaces need different settings
  • Working pattern: Wrong approach creates pooling and mess

Common DIY Mistakes

  • Using a turbo nozzle on delicate surfaces: Etches stone, damages concrete
  • Inconsistent distance: Creates light and dark patches
  • Working against slope: Water pools instead of draining
  • Skipping re-sanding: Block paving joints become unstable
  • Wrong chemicals: Acid on sandstone causes permanent damage

Professional Training Difference

Professional operators typically have:

  • Formal training on equipment and techniques
  • Experience with different surface types
  • Knowledge of which chemicals suit which materials
  • Understanding of drainage and water management
  • Skills to achieve even, streak-free results

Risk of Damage to Surfaces

This is where DIY can become expensive:

Common DIY Damage

  • Etching: Pressure too high creates permanent pitting in concrete and stone
  • Stripping: Removing paint from render, pointing from brickwork
  • Sand loss: Blasting jointing sand from block paving, destabilising blocks
  • Surface damage: Soft sandstone and old concrete are easily damaged
  • Water ingress: Forcing water under window seals, into walls, or through damaged pointing

Cost of Fixing Damage

  • Repointing block paving: £200-500
  • Repairing etched concrete: £300-800
  • Replacing damaged sandstone slabs: £50-150 per slab plus labour
  • Repairing render: £300-1000+
  • Damp treatment from water ingress: £500-3000+

Who Pays for Damage?

  • DIY: You pay for all damage - no insurance covers DIY mistakes
  • Professional: Covered by their public liability insurance (check they have it)

When DIY Hire Makes Sense

Despite the risks, there are situations where hiring makes sense:

Good Candidates for DIY

  • Small areas: Less than 20sqm - quick job, limited risk
  • Light soiling: Just dust and light dirt, no established moss
  • Robust surfaces: Modern concrete, tarmac, granite - hard to damage
  • Maintenance cleaning: Surface was professionally cleaned recently
  • You enjoy it: Some people genuinely find pressure washing satisfying
  • Learning investment: You plan to buy your own machine and this is practice

When You Have Time

If you have a free weekend and cleaning a driveway sounds like reasonable entertainment, DIY can work. But if you'd rather be doing something else, the time cost makes professional cleaning better value.

When Professional is Better Value

Clear Wins for Professional

  • Heavy moss/algae: Cold water hire machines don't kill roots - regrowth within months
  • Block paving: Proper re-sanding is essential and professionals include it
  • Indian sandstone/natural stone: Too easy to damage with wrong technique/products
  • Large areas: 40+ sqm is a full day's work with hired equipment
  • Time-poor: If your weekend is valuable, don't spend it pressure washing
  • Previous damage: If DIY has gone wrong before, don't repeat the mistake
  • Want lasting results: Hot water + professional technique = 18-24 months vs 6-12

The Time Value Calculation

Ask yourself: what else could you do with 6-8 hours?

  • If you work overtime at £20/hour, that's £120-160 of earning potential
  • If you'd spend it with family, what's that worth?
  • If you'd relax, is that worth the £50-100 difference?

Insurance Considerations

DIY Insurance

  • Home insurance: Generally doesn't cover DIY damage to your own property
  • Hire company insurance: Only covers damage to their equipment
  • Third party damage: If you damage a neighbour's property, your home insurance may or may not cover it
  • Personal injury: You're on your own for injuries during DIY

Professional Insurance

  • Public liability: Covers damage to your property during cleaning
  • Typical coverage: £1-5 million
  • Always ask: Confirm insurance before hiring any contractor
