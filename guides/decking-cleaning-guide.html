Slippery decking: the safety case

Cost aside, the strongest argument for regular cleaning is safety. RoSPA / HSE numbers:

  • 300,000 garden injuries/year (over 100,000 children)
  • 115,000 garden falls, slips, trips annually
  • 35% of slip injuries happen on wet surfaces, mostly Oct–Mar
  • At least 1 death/week from a slip, trip, or fall in the UK
  • Someone breaks a bone every 25 minutes from slips, trips, falls

Slippery decking is specifically cited by both RoSPA and HSE as a major hazard. Algae and moss thrive on damp wood and create a slick biofilm — invisible dry, dangerously slippery wet. In shaded gardens common across Surrey’s tree-lined suburbs, timber decking rarely dries out completely Oct–Mar.

Financial cost of injuries

  • Hip/pelvis from falls: £2,175–£76,350 compensation
  • Severe knee from falls: £15,500–£56,000
  • Total cost to UK employers: ~£512M/year
  • Total societal cost: ~£800M/year

£200–£400 for a pro deck clean is a fraction of the cost of a serious injury. With elderly relatives or young children using the garden, clean decking isn’t optional.

Cleaning methods

Pressure washing

  • Pressure-treated softwood: 1,000–1,200 PSI maximum
  • Cedar & soft softwoods: 500–600 PSI maximum
  • Composite: 500–1,000 PSI max, fan tip

Higher pressure raises the grain, splinters fibres, damages the surface. Pros use rotary surface cleaners for even coverage without the striping you get from a lance. £150–£350 for a pro wash.

Soft washing / chemical

Gentler alternative for fragile or older timber. Pre-treat with sodium percarbonate (oxy bleach), gentle rinse, brightener or neutraliser. Kills organisms at the root without mechanical force. Pre-treatment dwell 5–10 minutes for heavy soiling.

Sanding

Needed after pressure washing to smooth raised fibres, before re-staining, and on heavily weathered boards. 80–120 grit, work along the grain.

Full restoration sequence

  1. Clear furniture, sweep debris
  2. Chemical pre-treatment (kill algae and moss at root)
  3. Gentle pressure wash or soft wash
  4. Dry 24–48 hours
  5. Light sanding to smooth
  6. Apply oil/stain (1–2 coats per product instructions)

£10–£20/m² full restoration is the most comprehensive service — best for neglected timber not maintained for years.

Decking cleaning FAQs

The questions Surrey customers ask most often.

How much does decking cleaning cost in the UK?

From £5/m², ~£145 minimum. Small (5–10 m²): £100–£200. Medium (10–20): £200–£400. Large (20–40): £350–£500+. Add oil/stain and roughly double those.

How often should decking be cleaned?

At least once a year, ideally early spring (March–April). Best practice is twice a year, spring and autumn. High-traffic decks 3–4 times. Shaded or damp areas more often. Composite decking 2–3 times a year.

Is slippery decking actually dangerous?

Yes — serious slip hazard. RoSPA reports 300,000 UK garden injuries per year, with 115,000 falls, slips, trips. 35% of slip injuries are on wet surfaces (Oct–Mar). Compensation £2,175–£76,350 for serious cases.

Timber vs composite maintenance — what’s the difference?

Timber needs annual deep clean, sand, 1–2 coats oil/stain — £40–£80/application for 16 m² plus a weekend. Over 25 years that’s £500–£750+ products plus likely replacement at year 12–18. Composite needs occasional soap-and-brush wash — ~£10/year products, no oil, no sand, no stain.

Can you pressure wash composite decking?

Yes, with caution. Low setting (500–1,000 PSI maximum). Fan tip nozzle, 30cm minimum from surface, work with the grain. Higher pressure damages the surface, voids warranties, creates rough texture that traps dirt. For routine cleaning, soapy water and a soft brush is usually plenty.

How long does decking last with and without maintenance?

With maintenance: pressure-treated softwood 10–15 yrs, hardwood 25–50 yrs, capped composite 25–50 yrs. Without: softwood 5–10, hardwood 15–20, capped composite 20–30. Most UK timber decks die year 12–18. Untreated timber can warp and rot within 2–3 years.

What’s the best decking oil in the UK?

Which? rates Cuprinol Anti-Slip Decking Stain highly (2.5L from £32.99, 12–14 m²/L). Ronseal Quick Drying (2.5L £24.99) is the budget pick. Cuprinol 5L (~£33) is best value. Liberon Superior 5L (£41) is another strong performer. Always clean and fully dry the deck before applying.

When’s the best time to clean decking in the UK?

Early spring (March–April). Restores after winter, prepares for summer use. Above 10°C day and night for oil/stain to cure properly. Second clean in early autumn (Sep–Oct) clears leaves before the wet season. Avoid late autumn or winter — too cold for cure, too wet for drying.
