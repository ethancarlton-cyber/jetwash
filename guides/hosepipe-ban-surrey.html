One line worth sitting with: the reason our area is fine isn’t luck, it’s geology. SES Water pulls roughly 85% of its supply from underground aquifers in the chalk and greensand, with only 15% from its reservoir at Bough Beech[2]. Aquifers refill slowly and drain slowly, so one scorching June doesn’t empty them the way it empties a reservoir. That’s why Kent gets a ban and Redhill doesn’t, twenty-odd miles apart.

What changes if a ban arrives here

Worth knowing what a Temporary Use Ban actually restricts, so the headlines don’t catch you out. Under section 76 of the Water Industry Act 1991, a ban stops domestic hosepipe use: watering the garden or plants with a hosepipe, washing the car or boat, filling pools, ponds and fountains, cleaning walls or windows of your house, and cleaning paths, patios and other artificial outdoor surfaces[4]. Breaking it is a criminal offence with a fine of up to £1,000.

Two things people miss. First, it’s the hosepipe that’s banned, not the water: a bucket and a watering can stay legal, and so does kit fed from a water butt or tank. Second, the ban is written for householders, businesses using water for their day-to-day trade are generally exempt[7]. The full rules, the fines, and the professional exemption are all unpacked in our pressure washing during a hosepipe ban guide.

Surrey hosepipe ban FAQs

The questions coming in since the Kent ban made the news.

Is there a hosepipe ban in Redhill or Reigate?

No. Redhill and Reigate are supplied by SES Water, which has no hosepipe ban in place and none planned. Around 85 per cent of SES Water supply comes from underground aquifers rather than reservoirs, which is why the area is holding up while reservoir-fed neighbours bring in restrictions. We re-check the position weekly through the summer and update this page.

Which water company covers my postcode?

Most of our patch is SES Water: Redhill, Reigate, Merstham, Salfords, Earlswood, Horley, Caterham, Oxted, Godstone, Dorking, Leatherhead, Epsom and Banstead all sit inside its supply area. Crawley is Southern Water. If you are on the edge of a village, the name on your water bill is the final word, and every supplier has a postcode checker on its website.

Is Crawley affected by the Southern Water hosepipe ban?

No. Southern Water's current ban covers Hampshire and the Isle of Wight, not its Sussex supply area, so Crawley has no restrictions right now. It is the same company though, so if the dry weather carries on the ban could be extended. We keep this page updated weekly, so check back before you plan a big garden job.

Will SES Water bring in a hosepipe ban this summer?

Nothing is planned. SES Water draws most of its supply from underground aquifers, and Surrey's water companies have said resources are in a good place for 2026. A long enough drought can always change the picture, which is why we re-verify this page every week rather than leaving a stale answer up.

Can I still book a jet wash during a hosepipe ban?

Yes, in almost every case. Hosepipe bans restrict domestic hosepipe use, and professional cleaning is generally exempt as day-to-day business use, though each water company writes its own rules, so the specific ban notice is always worth a read. And in the Redhill and Reigate area there is no ban at all, so nothing changes here either way.
