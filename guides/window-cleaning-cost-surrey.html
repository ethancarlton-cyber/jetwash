Why the South East Sits at the Upper End

National cost guides quote a wide UK range, but where you live shifts your real price. London and the South East run roughly 25-50% higher than provincial rates because of the higher cost of doing business (WindowCleaningToday 2026). For a full first/one-off clean, that means a semi can land at £120-£180 in London and the South East versus £75-£110 in the North.

The good news for Surrey homeowners is that a local round-based crew is usually more competitive than a London firm, because travel and overheads are lower and the round keeps each visit efficient. You get South East quality without paying central-London prices.

Why a First Clean Costs More

The first time we clean a set of windows that have been left for months or years, we are removing far more than a regular visit. There is baked-on traffic film, pollen residue, green algae on the frames and water spotting from rain. That extra work is why a first or one-off clean costs 50-100% more than a maintained visit.

It is a one-time premium, not a recurring one. Once the windows are on a cycle, the build-up never comes back, so every clean after the first is a quick, low-cost touch-up. That is the whole logic of a round, and it is why committing to a regular cycle typically saves 15-30% per visit versus repeat one-offs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does window cleaning cost in Surrey?

For a typical Surrey home, a regular exterior clean is usually around £18-£40 per visit depending on size and number of windows. Flats and terraces sit at the lower end, three-bed semis in the middle, and detached homes at the top. A first or one-off clean costs 50-100% more because of built-up dirt. The South East sits at the upper end of national prices.

Why is window cleaning more expensive in the South East?

Window cleaning prices in London and the South East run roughly 25-50% higher than provincial rates because of higher costs of doing business. That puts Surrey at the upper end of UK price bands, though a local round-based crew is usually more competitive than a London firm because travel and overheads are lower.

How much does conservatory roof cleaning cost?

Conservatory roof cleaning typically costs £150-£400 depending on size and style, roughly £8-£12 per panel. A small lean-to runs £150-£250 and a large Victorian or Edwardian roof £300-£400. It is often bundled with a window clean since the crew is already on site.

Does a regular round save money on window cleaning?

Yes. Committing to a 4-, 6- or 8-weekly round typically works out 15-30% cheaper per visit than booking one-off cleans, because maintained glass has far less build-up to remove. The first clean is the only premium-priced visit; everything after is a faster, cheaper touch-up.

Are frames and sills included in the price?

Yes. With the pure-water pole the brush cleans the frames, sills and doors at the same time as the glass, every visit, at no extra charge. There are no hidden extras on a standard exterior clean.
