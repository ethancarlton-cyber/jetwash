How to Clean a Resin Driveway: UK Maintenance Guide

Gentle methods, the right products, and a maintenance schedule to keep your resin looking new.

14 min read · Updated February 2026

Get a free quote Call 01737 652 515

The quick answer: Clean a resin driveway by sweeping off loose debris, applying a resin-safe detergent or mild soapy water, scrubbing gently with a soft brush, and rinsing with a garden hose or low-pressure washer set to a maximum of 1500 PSI (100 bar). Never use solvents, strong acids, full-strength bleach, or high-pressure turbo nozzles — these damage the resin binder and dislodge aggregate stones. Regular gentle cleaning keeps resin driveways looking excellent for 15-25 years.

Resin bound driveways have become one of the most popular premium driveway surfaces in the UK, valued for their smooth finish, permeability, and low maintenance requirements. However, "low maintenance" doesn't mean "no maintenance." Resin driveways need the right cleaning approach — and crucially, there are several common products and methods that can cause serious damage. This guide covers the safe cleaning methods, what to avoid, how to handle stains, and a practical maintenance schedule.

What Is the Best Way to Clean a Resin Driveway?

Resin bound surfaces are more delicate than concrete, tarmac, or block paving. The resin binder holds individual aggregate stones in place, and aggressive cleaning can weaken this bond, dislodging stones and creating rough patches. Here are the recommended methods in order of gentleness.

Method 1: Soapy Water and Soft Brush (Best for Routine Cleaning)

For regular maintenance cleaning, this simple approach is all you need:

  • Mix warm water with a mild detergent (washing-up liquid works well)
  • Apply to the surface with a watering can or bucket
  • Scrub gently with a soft bristle broom or brush (not wire or stiff nylon)
  • Rinse thoroughly with a garden hose

This is safe for all resin surfaces and effective for light dirt, dust, and mild discolouration. It's the approach recommended by most resin driveway installers for regular upkeep.

Method 2: Biocide Treatment (Best for Algae and Moss)

For green algae or moss growth, a biocide treatment is more effective than scrubbing alone:

  • Apply a resin-safe biocide (check the label — avoid acid-based products)
  • Leave for the recommended time (usually 24-48 hours)
  • Gently scrub with a soft brush to loosen dead growth
  • Rinse with a hose or gentle pressure wash

Products like Algon, Wet & Forget, and diluted Jeyes Fluid (1:20 ratio) are generally safe for resin surfaces. Always test on a small, hidden area first. For comprehensive algae treatment advice, see our algae removal guide.

Method 3: Low-Pressure Washing (Best for Deeper Cleaning)

When scrubbing alone isn't enough, a pressure washer can be used carefully:

  • Maximum pressure: 1500 PSI (100 bar) — significantly lower than for concrete or tarmac
  • Nozzle: 40-degree fan nozzle or rotary surface cleaner only. NEVER use a turbo nozzle.
  • Distance: Keep the lance at least 30cm from the surface
  • Technique: Gentle sweeping motions, never hold in one spot
  • Water temperature: Cold or lukewarm only — hot water can soften the resin binder

What Should You Never Use on a Resin Driveway?

This is arguably the most important section of this guide. Using the wrong products or methods on resin causes damage that's expensive or impossible to repair.

Products to Avoid

  • Solvents: White spirit, turpentine, acetone, paint thinner — these dissolve the resin binder
  • Undiluted bleach: Discolours the resin and can weaken the binder. If using bleach for moss, dilute at least 1:20 and rinse thoroughly.
  • Acid-based cleaners: Muriatic acid, hydrochloric acid, and acidic patio cleaners attack the resin matrix
  • De-icing salt: Causes surface erosion over repeated applications. Use sand or cat litter for traction in icy conditions instead.
  • Petrol or diesel: These dissolve resin on contact. If a fuel spill occurs, absorb immediately with cat litter — don't wash it around the surface.

Methods to Avoid

  • Turbo or zero-degree nozzles: The concentrated jet dislodges aggregate instantly
  • Pressure above 1500 PSI: Progressively breaks the resin-aggregate bond
  • Wire brushes or metal scrapers: Scratch and gouge the surface, creating rough patches
  • Steam cleaners at high temperature: Excessive heat softens the resin binder
  • Power washing too close: Even at low pressure, holding the lance too close concentrates force on a small area

How Do You Remove Stains from a Resin Driveway?

Resin's semi-porous structure means stains can penetrate if not addressed quickly. Here's how to handle the most common stains.

Oil and Fuel Stains

The most critical stains to treat immediately, as fuel can dissolve the resin binder:

  • Fresh spills: Cover immediately with cat litter or sand. Do NOT try to wash the oil across the surface — this spreads the damage. Leave cat litter for 2-4 hours, sweep up, and treat the residual mark with a resin-safe degreaser.
  • Set-in stains: Apply a mild degreaser (avoid solvent-based products), leave for 15-20 minutes, scrub gently with a soft brush, and rinse. May need 2-3 applications.

For more oil removal techniques across different surfaces, see our oil stain removal guide.

Moss and Algae

Resin driveways are less prone to algae than porous surfaces like sandstone, but shaded areas can still develop green growth:

  • Apply a resin-safe biocide and leave for 24-48 hours
  • Scrub gently with a soft brush
  • Rinse with a hose or low-pressure wash
  • Apply a preventative biocide every 6-12 months to inhibit regrowth

Leaf Stains (Tannin Marks)

Fallen leaves left on resin driveways can leave brown tannin stains, especially from oak and chestnut trees:

  • Prevention is best — sweep leaves regularly, especially in autumn
  • For existing stains, apply a mild bleach solution (1:20) and scrub gently
  • Rinse thoroughly and repeat if needed
  • Stubborn tannin stains may fade naturally with UV exposure over several weeks

Tyre Marks

Hot tyres (after a long drive) can leave marks on resin, particularly on lighter aggregate:

  • Scrub with warm soapy water and a soft brush — most tyre marks come off easily
  • For persistent marks, use a resin-safe cleaner and gentle scrubbing
  • Avoid harsh chemicals — tyre marks are cosmetic and fade naturally over time

How Does UV Damage Affect Resin Driveways?

UV radiation from sunlight is one of the main long-term challenges for resin driveways. Over time, UV causes the resin binder to yellow, which is most noticeable on lighter-coloured aggregate. Here's what to know:

  • Timeframe: Yellowing typically becomes noticeable after 5-10 years, depending on UV exposure
  • Most affected: South-facing driveways in open positions with no shade. Lighter aggregate colours show yellowing more than darker ones.
  • Modern resins: UV-stable polyurethane resins resist yellowing much better than older epoxy formulations. If your driveway was installed after 2015, it likely uses UV-stable resin.
  • Prevention: A UV-resistant topcoat can be applied every 3-5 years to protect the binder. This costs £2-5 per sqm professionally.

Some colour change is natural and doesn't affect the structural integrity of the surface. If yellowing bothers you, a professional topcoat application restores appearance and provides ongoing protection.

Resin Driveway Maintenance Schedule

Following a regular maintenance schedule keeps your resin driveway looking excellent and extends its lifespan well beyond the typical 15-25 year expectation.

Frequency Task Time Required
Every 2-4 weeks Sweep or leaf blow to remove debris and leaves 5-10 minutes
Every 3 months Gentle wash with soapy water and soft brush 20-30 minutes
Every 6 months Inspect for algae growth, treat with biocide if needed 10-15 minutes
Annually Professional deep clean (recommended, not essential) 2-3 hours (professional)
Every 3-5 years UV-resistant topcoat application (professional) Half day (professional)
As needed Spot-treat oil spills immediately 15-30 minutes

Seasonal Tips

  • Spring: Clean thoroughly after winter to remove grit, salt residue, and any winter algae. This is the ideal time for a professional deep clean.
  • Summer: Minimal maintenance needed. Sweep regularly and spot-clean as required. Check for UV yellowing on lighter aggregates.
  • Autumn: Sweep leaves frequently — even daily during peak leaf fall. Tannin stains from decomposing leaves are the biggest autumn concern.
  • Winter: Avoid using de-icing salt. Use sand or cat litter for traction. If snow settles, clear with a plastic shovel (never metal).

Resin Bound vs Resin Bonded: Cleaning Differences

It's important to distinguish between the two types of resin driveway, as they require slightly different care:

  • Resin bound: Aggregate is mixed with resin before being laid, creating a smooth, permeable surface. This is the more common and premium option. Water drains through the surface. Clean gently as described above.
  • Resin bonded: Loose aggregate is scattered over a layer of resin applied to the base. The finish is rougher and more textured. It's less permeable and more prone to loose stones. Even gentler cleaning is required — very low pressure only, and avoid vigorous scrubbing that can dislodge poorly-bonded stones.

If you're unsure which type you have, look at the surface closely. Resin bound is smooth with stones evenly embedded. Resin bonded has a rougher, more uneven texture with stones sitting on top rather than surrounded by resin.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you pressure wash a resin driveway?

Yes, but use a maximum of 1500 PSI (100 bar) with a wide fan nozzle or rotary surface cleaner. Never use a turbo nozzle. Keep the lance at least 30cm away and use gentle sweeping motions. Too much pressure dislodges aggregate from the resin binder, creating bare patches.

How much does professional resin driveway cleaning cost?

Professional resin driveway cleaning costs £3-6 per sqm in the UK. A typical 40sqm driveway costs £120-240 for a standard clean. Adding a UV-resistant topcoat brings the cost to £5-10 per sqm. Ensure your cleaner has specific experience with resin bound surfaces.

How do you remove stains from a resin driveway?

For oil, absorb immediately with cat litter and treat with a resin-safe degreaser. For algae, apply a biocide and scrub gently. For leaf stains, use diluted bleach (1:20) and scrub. Never use solvents, strong acids, or full-strength bleach. Always test new products on a small hidden area first.

What should you not use on a resin driveway?

Never use solvents (white spirit, turpentine, acetone), acid cleaners, undiluted bleach, de-icing salt, wire brushes, turbo nozzles, or pressure above 1500 PSI. These damage the resin binder, dislodge aggregate, and cause discolouration that may require expensive repairs.

How often should you clean a resin driveway?

A gentle clean every 3 months and a deeper clean every 6-12 months keeps most resin driveways in excellent condition. Sweep every 2-4 weeks to prevent debris buildup. Properties under trees need more frequent sweeping to avoid leaf tannin stains.

Does UV damage affect resin driveways?

Yes, UV causes the resin binder to yellow gradually over 5-10 years, most noticeable on lighter aggregates. Modern UV-stable resins resist this better than older formulations. A UV-resistant topcoat applied every 3-5 years (£2-5/sqm) helps maintain the original colour and protects the binder.
