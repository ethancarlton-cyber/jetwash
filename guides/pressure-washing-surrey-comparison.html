What to Look for When Choosing a Pressure Washing Company

With dozens of options in Surrey, knowing what separates a good pressure washing company from a risky one is essential. Here are the seven factors that matter most.

1. Insurance and Public Liability Cover

This is non-negotiable. Any pressure washing company working on your property should carry public liability insurance of at least £1 million. This protects you if they accidentally damage your property, your neighbour's property, or a vehicle parked nearby. Pressure washing involves high-pressure water that can dislodge loose materials, break windows, damage paintwork, and strip sealant — even in experienced hands, accidents happen.

Ask to see their certificate of insurance before work begins. Legitimate companies will provide this without hesitation. If a company can't or won't show you their insurance documentation, do not use them regardless of how good their price is.

2. Genuine Customer Reviews

Google reviews are the most reliable indicator of a company's actual quality. Look for companies with at least 20+ reviews and a rating of 4.5 or above. Read the detailed reviews, not just the star rating — they'll tell you about communication, punctuality, the quality of the finished result, and how the company handled any issues.

Be cautious of companies with very few reviews, reviews that all appeared at once, or reviews that read like they were written by the same person. Check Checkatrade and Bark as well for additional verified feedback.

3. Professional Equipment

There is an enormous difference between a domestic pressure washer (the kind you can buy from B&Q for £150) and a professional-grade machine. Commercial pressure washers operate at 3,000-4,000 PSI with hot water capability, surface cleaners (the rotating disc attachments that give even results), and proper downstream chemical injection systems.

A company turning up with a domestic Karcher and a garden hose will take three times as long and deliver half the quality. Ask what equipment they use. If they mention specific brands like Interpump, Honda, or Nilfisk, that's a good sign. If they can't tell you, that's a red flag.

4. Clear, Written Quotes

A reputable company will provide a clear, written quote that specifies exactly what's included: the area to be cleaned, the method to be used, whether re-sanding is included (for block paving), whether sealing is included, and the total cost with no hidden extras. Be wary of companies that won't commit to a price until after the work is done, or that quote over the phone without seeing the job.

Getting a written quote also protects you legally. If the company does poor work or charges more than agreed, a written quote gives you grounds for a complaint or claim.

5. Realistic Pricing

If a quote seems too good to be true, it usually is. Pressure washing involves expensive equipment, fuel for hot water, chemicals, insurance, travel, and skilled labour. A company quoting £50 to clean a 60m² driveway is either using a domestic machine, cutting corners on insurance, or planning to upsell you once they arrive.

Equally, the most expensive quote isn't always the best. Compare at least three quotes and look for the middle ground — a fair price from a company with good reviews, proper insurance, and professional equipment.

6. Satisfaction Guarantee

Many of the better companies in Surrey offer a satisfaction guarantee or will return to address any areas you're not happy with at no extra charge. This shows confidence in their work and gives you peace of mind. Ask about their guarantee policy before booking. Companies that stand behind their work are more likely to do it properly first time.

7. Communication and Professionalism

How a company handles your initial enquiry tells you a lot about how they'll handle the job. Do they respond promptly? Do they answer your questions clearly? Do they turn up for the quote at the agreed time? A company that's difficult to reach or vague in their communication before they've won the work is unlikely to improve once they have your money.

Questions to Ask Before Hiring

Before committing to any pressure washing company, ask these seven questions. The answers will quickly separate the professionals from the amateurs.

  1. "Can I see your public liability insurance certificate?" — Any legitimate company will show you this immediately. It should be current (check the dates) and show at least £1 million cover. If they hesitate, move on.
  2. "What equipment do you use?" — You want to hear about commercial-grade machines, surface cleaners, and hot water capability. If they mention a specific make and model, that's reassuring. If they can't name their equipment, that's concerning.
  3. "What cleaning method will you use on my surface?" — The right answer depends on your surface type. Block paving needs different treatment than concrete, which needs different treatment than natural stone. A good company will explain their approach and why it's appropriate for your specific surface.
  4. "Is re-sanding included for block paving?" — If you have block paving, this is essential. Pressure washing removes kiln-dried sand from the joints. If it's not replaced, weeds return faster and the blocks can shift. Some companies include this; others charge extra. Clarify before booking.
  5. "What does the price include, and are there any extras?" — Get a clear breakdown. Does it include weed treatment? Moss treatment? Sealing? Travel costs? Waste removal? The cheapest quote often becomes the most expensive once extras are added.
  6. "Do you offer a satisfaction guarantee?" — Good companies will guarantee their work and return to fix any issues. If a company won't stand behind their results, question why.
  7. "Can you provide references or examples of recent local work?" — Ask for before-and-after photos or references from recent jobs in your area. Most established companies have a portfolio of work they're proud to show.

How to Get the Best Value

Getting good value from pressure washing doesn't necessarily mean finding the cheapest price. It means getting the best results for your money and making those results last as long as possible. Here's how.

Book at the Right Time

Pressure washing companies are busiest in spring and early summer when homeowners want their outdoor spaces looking good for the warmer months. If you can be flexible on timing, booking during quieter periods — January, February, or mid-week slots — can sometimes save you 10-20%. Many companies offer discounts to fill their winter schedules.

That said, the best time to clean is when the surface actually needs it. Waiting an extra six months for a discount while your driveway becomes a slip hazard covered in green algae isn't good value.

Bundle Multiple Surfaces

If you need your driveway, patio, and paths all cleaned, get a single quote for the lot rather than booking each separately. Most companies offer a significant discount for larger jobs because the travel time, setup, and equipment preparation are the same whether they clean 30m² or 100m². A combined job that might cost £450 separately could be £300-350 as a single booking.

Consider Sealing After Cleaning

Sealing your driveway or patio after cleaning adds £3-8 per m² to the cost but extends the time before you need to clean again by 3-5 years. For block paving, a good polymeric sand and sealant combination also prevents weed growth and stabilises the blocks. The upfront cost pays for itself by reducing the frequency of future cleaning. See our DIY vs professional guide for more on the long-term value equation.

Get at Least Three Quotes

This cannot be overstated. Prices in Surrey can vary by 50-100% between companies for identical work. Getting three quotes helps you understand the fair market rate, identify any outliers (both suspiciously cheap and unreasonably expensive), and compare what's included. Use the quotes as an opportunity to ask the questions listed earlier in this guide.

Ask About Maintenance Plans

Some companies offer annual or bi-annual maintenance plans at a reduced rate. If your property is prone to moss and algae (north-facing driveways, shaded patios, areas near trees), a maintenance plan can be more cost-effective than booking individual cleans as and when you remember.

Don't Chase the Cheapest Quote

The cheapest quote often comes from an operator with minimal insurance, domestic equipment, and no guarantee. If they damage your block paving or strip the colour from your Indian sandstone, you'll have little recourse and the cost of repair will dwarf any savings on the cleaning. Choose a company with the right balance of fair pricing, good reviews, and professional credentials.

Yes, always get at least three quotes. Prices can vary by 50-100% between companies for the same job. Is it worth paying more for an experienced company?

Generally yes. Experienced operators (10+ years) know the correct pressure settings, nozzle choices, and chemical treatments for each surface type. Incorrect technique can damage block paving joints, strip colour from Indian sandstone, etch concrete, and force water behind render. The cost of repairing poorly done pressure washing often exceeds the original cleaning cost several times over.

What insurance should a pressure washing company have?

At minimum, £1 million public liability insurance. This covers accidental damage to your property, neighbouring properties, and vehicles during the work. Many reputable companies carry £2-5 million. Ask to see their certificate before work begins. Check that it's current and covers pressure washing specifically — some general liability policies exclude certain activities.

Most Surrey-based companies cover a 15-30 mile radius from their base. Key areas include Guildford, Woking, Redhill, Reigate, Dorking, Epsom, Leatherhead, Crawley, and Horley. Some London-based companies also serve northern Surrey including Croydon, Sutton, and Kingston. Always confirm your postcode is within their coverage area before booking.

When is the best time to book pressure washing?

Spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November) are ideal. Spring removes winter grime before the outdoor entertaining season. Autumn clears leaf stains and prepares surfaces for winter. Booking mid-week or during quieter months (January-February) can save 10-20% as companies fill their schedules. Avoid booking during heavy rain periods — wet conditions can dilute cleaning chemicals and extend drying times.
