How Much Does Render Cleaning Cost by House Size?

The size and type of your property is the biggest factor in render cleaning cost. Larger properties have more surface area, but access difficulties on multi-storey homes can also push prices higher. Here is what you can expect to pay for professional soft washing in 2026:

Property Type Surface Area Typical Cost
2-bed terrace (front only) 50–65m² £300–£400
3-bed semi-detached 80–100m² £500–£600
Detached house (full) 120–150m² £700–£850
Large detached / multi-storey 150–400m² £1,000–£2,500
Commercial building 400m²+ £2,500–£5,000+

These prices include biocide treatment and a gentle low-pressure rinse where required. For properties in Surrey and the South East, prices sit at the higher end of the national average due to higher operating costs and the affluent nature of the region. Properties in areas like Redhill, Reigate, and Dorking typically fall within these ranges.

Multi-storey properties may require scaffolding or cherry picker access, which adds significant cost. Always ask whether access equipment is included in a quote.

Render Cleaning Cost by Render Type

Different render systems require slightly different approaches, which affects pricing. The most common types found on UK properties are:

Monocouche Render (e.g., K-Rend K1)

Cost: ~£6.50 per m² (specialist soft wash required)

Monocouche render is the most popular choice for UK new-build developments due to its speed of application and built-in weatherproofing. K-Rend K1 is the dominant brand. Applied at approximately 9mm thickness in one or two passes, it has a distinctive scratched texture with visible render beads. This thin application makes it particularly vulnerable to pressure washing damage. Only specialist soft washing with approved biocides should be used.

Silicone Thin-Coat Render

Cost: £5–£7 per m²

Silicone thin-coat render is increasingly popular for higher-specification new builds and retrofit projects. Applied as a thin layer (approximately 1.5mm) over a reinforced basecoat with fibreglass mesh, it offers excellent breathability and self-cleaning properties. Despite being marketed as self-cleaning, it still accumulates biological growth in the UK's damp climate and requires periodic soft washing.

Acrylic Render

Cost: £5–£6 per m²

Acrylic render is a thin-coat system commonly found on budget new builds. It comes in a wide range of colours and textures. Like other modern renders, it must only be cleaned using soft wash methods — never high-pressure equipment.

Traditional Cement/Sand Render

Cost: £4–£6 per m²

Traditional cement render is found on older properties and extensions. Applied in multiple thicker coats and typically painted, it is more robust than modern thin-coat systems. While it can tolerate slightly more pressure than monocouche, soft washing is still recommended to avoid surface damage and to ensure biological growth is killed at the root.

Render Type Thickness Cost per m² Key Consideration

Silicone thin-coat ~1.5mm £5–£7 Very thin — extremely pressure-sensitive
Acrylic Thin £5–£6 Colour-sensitive, no bleach unless tested
Cement/sand (traditional) Thicker £4–£6 More robust but soft wash still recommended

Why Soft Washing Is Essential for Render

This is the most important section of this guide. If you take one thing away, let it be this: never use a standard pressure washer on modern render. The consequences range from cosmetic damage to complete render failure requiring thousands of pounds in repairs.

What Pressure Washing Does to Render

K-Rend and other monocouche renders are only approximately 9mm thick. A standard pressure washer operating at 2,000–3,000 PSI will:

  • Strip the surface layer: Taking even a few millimetres off each clean leads to cracks, pieces falling off, and an uneven finish
  • Drive moisture behind the render: High-pressure water penetrates through micro-cracks, causing damp problems and accelerating deterioration
  • Cause etching and pitting: The surface loses its smooth texture and water-repellent properties
  • Leave root organisms alive: Pressure washing removes visible growth but leaves spores of moss, algae, and mould behind — meaning stains return within months

One well-documented case saw a homeowner use a standard pressure washer on K-Rend render, resulting in £2,000+ in repair and reapplication costs. This is not unusual — render repair is expensive because entire sections often need stripping and reapplying to achieve an even finish.

How Soft Washing Works

Professional soft washing cleans render using chemical biocides applied at very low pressure — equivalent to a garden hose. The process follows a careful sequence:

  1. Assessment: The cleaner identifies the type of growth — green algae, black mould (Gloeocapsa magma), red Trentepohlia, or white efflorescence — as each requires a different treatment approach
  2. Preparation: Adjacent plants are pre-wetted and sensitive areas covered to protect them from biocide runoff
  3. Chemical application: Biocide solution is applied at low pressure using a water-fed pole and 12V pump, ensuring even coverage without surface damage
  4. Dwell time: Chemicals are left to work — 10–15 minutes for light staining, 20–30 minutes for moderate algae, and 45–60 minutes for heavy lichen or black spot
  5. Rinse: A gentle low-pressure rinse removes loosened growth if required
  6. Residual protection: The biocide continues working for weeks and months after application, providing ongoing protection against regrowth

What K-Rend Themselves Recommend

K-Rend officially advises that their render can be cleaned using a pressure washer with clean water and a low concentration of mild detergent — but on a fan application setting, not high power. They recommend light cleaning and maintenance dependent on weathering and atmospheric spore levels. K-Rend also offers K Pro, their own protective treatment that enhances water-repellent properties. The key takeaway: even the manufacturer warns against high-pressure cleaning.

Warning: Using high pressure or unapproved cleaners on modern render will void the manufacturer warranty (typically 10–15 years). Only professional soft washing maintains warranty protection.

Types of Render Staining and What They Mean

Understanding what's growing on your render helps you communicate with cleaning professionals and set realistic expectations for results. The UK's damp, mild climate is perfect for biological growth — algae and mould need moisture, shade, and organic deposits to thrive, and British weather provides all three in abundance.

Green Algae

The most common type of render staining. Green algae appears as a green film or slimy growth, predominantly on shaded and damp areas, particularly north-facing walls. It thrives wherever walls stay wet for extended periods. Green algae usually disappears within one hour of biocide treatment.

Red/Orange Algae (Trentepohlia)

Despite its red or orange appearance, Trentepohlia is actually a filamentous green algae that produces carotenoid pigments (the same compounds that make carrots orange). It appears as reddish-orange streaks or a powdery coating and favours damp, slightly acidic environments. After biocide treatment, it turns pink before disappearing entirely within 4–24 hours.

Black Staining (Gloeocapsa Magma)

Black vertical streaks running down your render are caused by Gloeocapsa magma, a blue-green bacterium. It colonises water run-off paths, particularly under gutters, window sills, and anywhere water drips consistently. Black staining normally disappears within two hours after a second treatment application. This is often the most stubborn type of staining and may require multiple treatment cycles.

White Deposits (Efflorescence)

White powdery coating on render is efflorescence — mineral salts drawn to the surface by moisture. This is common on new render and in areas with high moisture levels. Unlike biological growth, efflorescence is not caused by living organisms and may return if the underlying moisture issue is not resolved. Cleaning will remove the visible deposits, but addressing damp sources (leaking gutters, poor drainage) is essential for a lasting solution.

What Accelerates Staining

Several underlying issues can cause render to get dirty faster than normal:

  • Blocked or leaking gutters keeping walls wet longer than they should be
  • Bad drainage or pooling water at the base of walls
  • Walls that cannot dry out due to shade, poor airflow, or overgrown vegetation
  • Metal brackets (satellite dishes, hanging baskets) causing rust staining — K-Rend recommends stainless steel fixings only

In Surrey, properties face particular challenges. With approximately 133 rain days per year and annual rainfall of 722mm, walls rarely dry out completely during autumn and winter. Properties near the North Downs or surrounded by trees are especially vulnerable. North-facing walls in Surrey can remain damp for weeks at a time.

Biocide Treatments: What Professionals Use

The chemicals used in professional soft washing are the key to long-lasting results. Understanding what they are and how they work helps you evaluate quotes and ask the right questions.

DDAC (Didecyldimethylammonium Chloride)

DDAC is the workhorse of professional render cleaning. Derived from naturally occurring plant oils, it targets the underlying biofilm responsible for algae, lichen, fungi, and mould. Unlike bleach-based products, DDAC continues working for months after application — it "deep cleans over time," gradually eliminating organisms that bleach alone would miss. Popular UK products include Benz Bio Cleanze (50% DDAC, approximately £50 for 5 litres) and SoftWash Pro 50.

Sodium Hypochlorite (SH)

Sodium hypochlorite is a rapid-acting bleach-based biocide used for initial heavy cleaning. At 3–5% active chlorine concentration, it penetrates the holdfast structures of lichen and rhizoids of moss within 15–30 minutes, delivering visible results quickly. Products like Benz Lightning Cleanze (10–11% SH) are commonly used for first cleans on heavily stained render.

The Combined Approach

Most professional render cleaners use both products together: SH first for immediate visible improvement, followed by DDAC for long-term residual protection. This two-stage approach delivers the best of both worlds — instant results and lasting protection. The biocide chemicals themselves are relatively inexpensive — a typical domestic soft wash uses just £10–£30 of chemicals. The bulk of the professional cost covers labour, equipment, insurance, and expertise.

How Long Does Render Cleaning Last?

This is one of the most common questions homeowners ask, and the answer depends entirely on the method used. The contrast between professional soft washing and DIY pressure washing is stark:

Cleaning Method Duration Before Regrowth
Professional soft wash (with biocide) 2–5 years
Pressure wash only (no biocide) 3–12 months
Shaded / north-facing walls 1–2 years (shorter)
Exposed / south-facing walls 3–5 years (longer)
Coastal or tree-covered areas 1–3 years
With maintenance biocide top-up 5+ years

The general consensus among UK professionals is that most properties benefit from cleaning every 3–5 years, though shaded or coastal homes may need it more frequently. Applying a maintenance dose of DDAC biocide at the first sign of regrowth — typically 2–3 years after the initial clean on vertical surfaces — can extend protection significantly without requiring a full clean.

This is the fundamental economic argument for soft washing over pressure washing: spending £500–£800 every 3–5 years is dramatically cheaper than spending £200–£400 every year on pressure washing that damages your render with each application.

DIY Render Cleaning: Why the Risks Outweigh the Savings

The temptation to save money by cleaning render yourself is understandable. A professional soft wash for a 3-bed semi costs £500–£600, while a pressure washer can be hired for £50–£80 a day. However, the risks are substantial and the potential costs of getting it wrong far exceed the savings.

Physical Damage

  • Standard pressure washers cause etching, surface pitting, and permanent loss of water-repellent properties
  • Render starts absorbing water and discolouring unevenly after damage — this is irreversible without full reapplication
  • Documented repair cost: £2,000+ for stripping and reapplying render after pressure washer damage
  • Many retail cleaning products are not designed for render and can bleach patches, streak colour, or corrode the surface

Warranty Implications

  • K-Rend and other modern renders carry manufacturer warranties of 10–15 years
  • Using high pressure or unapproved cleaners will void the warranty
  • Only certified render cleaners can guarantee warranty protection is maintained
  • A single improper clean could invalidate a decade of warranty protection

Safety Risks

  • Working at height without training or proper equipment
  • Chemical handling without PPE or knowledge of correct dilution ratios
  • Risk of damaging windows, UPVC frames, and adjacent surfaces with incorrectly mixed chemicals

Before and After: What to Expect

Setting realistic expectations helps avoid disappointment. Professional render cleaning delivers excellent results, but some outcomes are immediate while others develop over weeks.

What Professional Cleaning CAN Fix

  • Green algae: Usually disappears within 1 hour of treatment
  • Red algae (Trentepohlia): Turns pink, then disappears within 4–24 hours
  • Black staining: Normally disappears within 2 hours after second treatment
  • General weathering/dullness: Render appears bright and years younger
  • Moss and lichen: Killed at the root, falls away over days or weeks

What Professional Cleaning CANNOT Fix

  • Cracking or structural damage: Cleaning will not repair physical defects in the render
  • Colour fading from UV damage: The original pigment may have degraded beyond restoration
  • Damage from previous pressure washing: Etching and pitting are permanent
  • Recurring efflorescence: May return if the underlying moisture source is not resolved
  • Rust staining from non-stainless fixings: Requires specific treatment and replacement of the fixings

Important Timing Notes

Results are not always instant. Some biological growth clears gradually over weeks as the biocide continues working. Deep-rooted black fungi may need multiple treatment visits. A biocide-treated wall continues to self-clean for months after the professional visit, so the render may actually look better a month later than it did on the day of cleaning.

Render Cleaning Cost Factors: What Affects Your Price

Several variables can push your quote higher or lower than the averages listed above:

Severity of Staining

Light organic staining requires less chemical product and shorter dwell times than heavy lichen or black mould. A lightly soiled property may cost 20–30% less than one with severe biological growth requiring multiple treatment passes.

Access and Height

Single-storey properties are straightforward. Two-storey homes can usually be reached with water-fed poles. Three-storey or complex properties may require scaffolding or cherry picker hire, which adds significantly to the cost. Properties with limited parking, narrow access, or no external water supply may also incur small surcharges.

Location

South East England prices are higher than the national average due to higher operating costs. Urban areas like London and Surrey face additional challenges — parking restrictions, congestion charges, and higher insurance premiums all contribute. Rural properties may have lower base rates but longer travel distances can offset this.

Property Orientation

North-facing walls and shaded elevations accumulate more biological growth than exposed south-facing walls. If only certain elevations need cleaning, some professionals offer per-elevation pricing rather than whole-house rates.

Red Flags on Pricing

Anything below £2 per m² should raise suspicion. Extremely cheap quotes often indicate operators without proper insurance, training, or the correct equipment. They may use high-pressure washing (damaging your render) or cheap, ineffective chemicals that provide only temporary results.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does render cleaning cost in the UK?

Render cleaning costs £5–£8 per square metre in the South East in 2026. A 2-bed terrace costs £300–£400 (front only), a 3-bed semi costs £500–£600, and a detached house costs £700–£850. Prices depend on render type, severity of staining, access requirements, and your location.

Why can't you pressure wash K-Rend or monocouche render?

K-Rend monocouche render is only approximately 9mm thick. High-pressure washing strips the surface, causes cracking, drives moisture behind the render, and removes its water-repellent properties. One documented case resulted in £2,000+ in repair costs. Pressure washing also fails to kill root organisms, meaning staining returns within 3–12 months.

How long does professional render cleaning last?

A professional soft wash with biocide treatment lasts 2–5 years on most properties. South-facing walls can stay clean for 3–5 years, while shaded north-facing walls may need retreatment in 1–2 years. Applying a maintenance biocide at the first sign of regrowth can extend protection to 5+ years.

What causes the green, red, and black stains on render?

Green stains are common algae. Red or orange stains come from Trentepohlia, a filamentous algae with carotenoid pigments. Black vertical streaks are Gloeocapsa magma, a bacterium that colonises water run-off paths under gutters. White powdery deposits (efflorescence) are mineral salts from moisture.

Can I clean render myself?

DIY render cleaning is risky. Homeowners using pressure washers regularly cause etching, pitting, and loss of water-repellent properties, with documented repair costs exceeding £2,000. Unapproved cleaners or high pressure will void manufacturer warranties (typically 10–15 years). Professional soft washing uses specialist biocides at garden-hose pressure, which is far safer and more effective.

What chemicals do professionals use to clean render?

Professionals use DDAC (Didecyldimethylammonium chloride) for long-term protection and Sodium Hypochlorite for rapid stain removal. Popular UK products include Benz Bio Cleanze, Benz Lightning Cleanze, and AlgoClear Pro. The chemicals cost just £10–£30 per domestic job — the bulk of the cost covers labour, equipment, and insurance.

Will render cleaning void my warranty?

It depends on the method. High-pressure washing or unapproved chemicals will void K-Rend and most modern render warranties. Professional soft washing with approved biocides at low pressure maintains warranty protection. Always ask whether your cleaner is familiar with your specific render system.

What is the best time of year to clean render?

Spring (March–May) and early autumn (September–October) are ideal. Biocides need mild temperatures to work effectively, and surfaces should be dry enough for chemicals to absorb. Avoid freezing conditions. In Surrey, with 133 rain days per year, scheduling around dry spells is important.
