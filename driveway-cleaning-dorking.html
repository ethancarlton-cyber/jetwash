Why Dorking drives green up faster, and what a maintenance clean costs

This is the honest bit most cleaners won’t tell you. Dorking sits at the bottom of the Mole Gap, the cut the river made through the North Downs, sheltered by Box Hill and the chalk to the north and the Greensand ridge towards Westcott and Leith Hill to the south[2]. That shelter is lovely to live in and terrible for driveways: it slows the wind, traps cold damp air on the valley floor overnight, and the Surrey Hills tree canopy keeps direct sun off the surface. The Met Office rain figure at Wisley is the same as the rest of east Surrey[1], but your drive stays wet for weeks longer each year, and damp is exactly what algae, black spot and moss feed on.

The practical result: an RH4 or RH5 drive that greens over in the shaded band by the pavement within a year, where the same drive up on drier ground might go two. So rather than sell you a full deep clean every time, we’ll often suggest a lighter maintenance clean once your drive is on a regular cycle. There’s far less moss to pre-treat and less time on the machine, so a single maintenance drive can start at the £80 minimum and a double often lands around £120 to £160, well under the cost of letting it go feral and paying for the heavy job again. We’ll tell you honestly which one your drive needs when we look, and there’s more on timing in how often to clean a driveway.

Block paving, tarmac, concrete and resin drives in Dorking

What the microclimate means for each surface across RH4 and RH5:

  • Block paving on the estates. The interwar and post-war streets around North Holmwood, Bentsbrook and Chart Downs, and the newer closes off Westcott Road, are mostly block paving. The joints are the weak point: sand washes out, moss and weeds move in, and here the Mole Valley damp refills those joints faster than anywhere else on our patch. We wash, kill the weeds and re-sand with kiln dried sand as standard. More on the surface itself on our block paving cleaning page, and on the weeds in weeds in block paving.
  • Concrete and tarmac in the town centre. The Georgian and Victorian terraces off West Street, Dene Street and the High Street mostly have small concrete or tarmac forecourts. Quick jobs, usually at or near the £80 minimum, and the difference between grey-green and its original colour is startling.
  • Resin-bound drives. A fair few of the newer Dorking and Pixham builds have resin. It cleans up well but hates high pressure up close, the binder can scar, so we run lower pressure and more chemistry. We price these on a photo or a quick look first.
  • Sloped drives off the hills. Dorking is anything but flat. Drives running down off Box Hill, Ranmore and the Deepdene slopes drain fast at the top and hold water at the bottom, so the moss concentrates in a band near the road. Normal here, and it comes off the same.

What’s included in a Dorking driveway clean

  1. Walk the drive first. Surface, condition, drainage, oil stains, anything loose or cracked. If a repair is what you actually need, we say so rather than wash over the problem.
  2. Pre-treat. Moss and algae get a sodium hypochlorite treatment before the water goes anywhere near it. The dilution and dwell time do the work, not brute pressure, and on a shaded Mole Valley drive that pre-treat earns its keep. More in our algae removal guide.
  3. Machine wash. Rotary surface cleaner across the field of the drive for an even, stripe-free finish, then the lance for edges, corners and the awkward bits by the garage door.
  4. Weeds and joints. Weeds cleared from the joints, and on block paving we re-sand with kiln dried sand once the surface has dried off. Included, always.
  5. Tidy up. Slurry and debris bagged and taken away, paths and gullies rinsed down. We leave the drive clean and the rest of the frontage no worse than we found it.

Optional extras, priced honestly: oil stain treatment (some stains never fully lift from concrete, we’ll tell you before we start) and sealing after the clean. Plenty of Surrey drives don’t need sealing at all, so we’ll only quote it where it earns its keep.

Why driveway quotes vary

Two Dorking drives the same size can be £100 apart, and the reasons are boring but real: surface type, condition, access, and whether re-sanding or sealing is in scope. In the Mole Valley, condition is the big one, a drive left three years greens up far worse here than the same drive in a drier town, so it takes longer and costs more.
