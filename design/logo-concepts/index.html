JetWash — Logo Concepts

3 concepts, each shown on a white card and a dark navy card.

Current logo assessment

The current header mark (index.html lines 85-90, styled in css/v2.css .brand__mark) is a 32x32px dark-navy (#0B1B2B / --ink) rounded square containing a single 18x18px water-droplet <path> filled in blue accent (#1E6FE5 / --accent), sitting next to a plain weight-800 "Same Day Jetwash" wordmark. Problems: (1) Generic — a lone teardrop reads as any plumbing/water/weather brand and says nothing about pressure-washing, exteriors, or the "same day" promise; (2) the icon is identical to a thousand stock water icons, so it carries zero brand equity; (3) at the rendered 18px the droplet inside a dark box has weak figure-ground contrast and just looks like a smudge at small sizes; (4) the wordmark is undifferentiated flat text with no typographic hierarchy distinguishing "Same Day" (the USP) from "Jetwash" (the service); (5) favicons (favicon.ico / 16/32 / apple-touch) appear to be a legacy asset disconnected from this mark, so the brand is inconsistent across surfaces. Net: weak, forgettable, and not legible/distinct at the small size the header demands. The fixes below keep the existing navy (#0B1B2B) + blue (#1E6FE5) palette, use only system/web-safe fonts, and give each concept a clearer "fast exterior cleaning" idea plus a two-weight wordmark that separates the USP from the service.

1. Jet Sweep

A spray-nozzle/jet-motion mark: a compact navy rounded tile holds a stylised lance tip firing three tapered blue jet lines that sweep right into a clean arc, literally depicting pressure-washing in action and the forward 'same day' momentum. It is bolder and more legible at 18-32px than a lone droplet because the diagonal jet lines hold their shape when tiny. Uses only navy #0B1B2B and accent blue #1E6FE5; wordmark splits the USP 'Same Day' (lighter weight) from 'Jetwash' (heavy) for hierarchy. system-ui font keeps it dependency-free.

2. Clean Droplet

Keeps the water-droplet idea (familiar, on-theme for exterior cleaning) but fixes it: the droplet is rendered as a clean blue outline on a transparent/light ground rather than buried in a dark box, with a subtle 'sparkle'/shine cut-out that signals 'clean/spotless' — the actual outcome the business sells. Open and light, it stays crisp and high-contrast at favicon size. Two-tone navy+blue, no fill-on-dark muddiness. Wordmark stacks 'Same Day' over a heavy 'Jetwash' for instant hierarchy.

3. Pure Wordmark + Accent Bar

The most modern/minimal route: no pictorial icon at all, just a confident wordmark with a single vertical accent bar as the brand 'mark'. 'Same Day' sits small and blue above a heavy navy 'JETWASH'; the blue bar doubles as a favicon-able monogram and a subtle 'jet of water' vertical streak. Maximally legible at any size (it is just type), trivially scalable, and the cleanest fit for a trustworthy local-service brand. Pure navy + blue, system font, zero raster dependency.
