Common Patio Problems in Surrey

Black Spots on Patios

Black spots are one of the most frustrating patio problems for homeowners in Redhill, Reigate and surrounding areas. These dark stains are typically caused by Lichen planus, a fungus that embeds itself in porous patio materials. Our specialist treatment targets these black spots effectively, removing them and preventing rapid regrowth.

Slippery Algae Growth

Surrey's damp climate creates perfect conditions for green algae to flourish on patios, especially in shaded areas. This creates dangerous slippery surfaces that are hazardous to walk on. Our pressure washing removes all algae growth, making your patio safe for family use.

Moss Between Paving

Moss loves to grow in the joints between patio slabs, creating an unkempt appearance and potentially lifting slabs if left unchecked. We thoroughly remove moss and can provide re-pointing services if required. Our moss removal guide covers effective prevention methods for all outdoor surfaces.

General Dirt & Staining

Over time, patios accumulate dirt, leaf stains, bird droppings, and general weathering that make them look dull and neglected. Professional cleaning restores the original appearance dramatically.

Frequently Asked Questions About Patio Cleaning

How do you remove black spots from patio slabs?

Black spots on patios are caused by lichen, a stubborn fungus that embeds into porous stone. We use specialist biocide treatments to kill the lichen at root level, followed by careful pressure washing. This removes the black spots effectively and slows regrowth compared to pressure washing alone.

Is pressure washing safe for Indian stone patios?

Yes, when done correctly. Indian stone requires careful pressure washing at appropriate settings to avoid surface damage. We adjust our equipment specifically for Indian stone, cleaning thoroughly while preserving the stone's natural beauty and avoiding etching or pitting. Read our DIY vs professional comparison to understand why expert technique matters.

Should I seal my patio after cleaning?

Sealing after cleaning is optional but recommended for many patio types. Sealant protects against future staining, makes cleaning easier, and can enhance the stone's colour. We offer sealing as an optional extra service, particularly beneficial for sandstone, limestone, and other porous natural stones.

How much does patio cleaning cost in Surrey?

Patio cleaning in Surrey typically costs £3-7 per square metre, depending on the patio material, size, and condition. A typical 20-30 sqm patio costs around £80-180. Black spot removal may cost slightly more due to the specialist treatment required. View our pricing guide or request a free quote.

How long before I can use my patio after cleaning?

Your patio is safe to walk on immediately after cleaning, though we recommend waiting 24-48 hours before placing furniture back to allow complete drying. If sealing is applied, you'll need to keep the patio dry and foot-traffic free for at least 24 hours for the sealant to cure properly.
