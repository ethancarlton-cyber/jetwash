## F-001 — Surrey is significantly DRIER than UK average (corrects "wet UK" framing)

**Established**: 2026-05-20 (Cycle 1)
**Source**: Met Office Wisley (Surrey) Location Long-Term Averages 1991-2020 — https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/research/climate/maps-and-data/location-specific-long-term-averages/gcpevmgzn
**Data**: Wisley (closest Met Office station to Redhill/Reigate RH1) records **648.41 mm/year** rainfall (1991-2020 30-year average). UK national average for same period ≈ 1147 mm. Surrey is therefore **~43% drier than UK average**.

**Why it's counterintuitive**: Almost every UK pressure-washing guide opens with "in our wet UK climate, moss returns quickly." For most of England that's true. For Surrey specifically it is misleading — Wisley is one of the driest Met Office stations in the south of England. RH1 drives can extend cleaning intervals roughly 6 months longer than the typical UK guide implies.

## F-002 — The legitimate UK driveway cleaning price floor is ~£3/m² (sub-floor implies corner-cutting)

**Established**: 2026-05-20 (Cycle 1)
**Sources**:
- ONS / DBT NLW £12.71/hr from 1 April 2026 (gov.uk)
- Thames Water 2026/27 tariff £4.21/m³ combined (thameswater.co.uk)
- Kärcher K7 manufacturer spec 600 L/hr (kaercher.com)
- Phoenix Gray UK construction labour rates 2026 (skilled tradesperson £22-40/hr range)

**Data**: A first-principles build-up for a 40m² block paving driveway in Surrey 2026:
- Labour ~2 hrs @ £30/hr = £60
- Water (Karcher K7 @ 600 L/hr × 2 hrs × £4.21/m³) = £5.05
- Consumables (kiln-dried sand + biocide) ≈ £8
- Insurance + overhead amortised ≈ £8
- Direct cost: ~£81 (~£2.03/m²)
- Add 25% margin: ~£101 (~£2.53/m²)
- **Realistic market floor: £3/m² (£120 for 40m²)**

**Why it's counterintuitive**: Most UK cost guides report £3-£6/m² as the "average" range, implying £3 is the low end of a normal market. Our build-up shows £3/m² is the *price floor* — anything below means the cleaner is either uninsured, skipping pre-treatment / surface cleaner / re-sand, or paying themselves below minimum wage.

## F-003 — Marshalls cleaning guidance: medium pressure only, 30° lance angle, 200mm minimum standoff

**Established**: 2026-05-20 (Cycle 1)
**Source**: Marshalls Garden Paving & Driveways Cleaning & Maintenance Guidelines — https://media.marshalls.co.uk/image/upload/v1552901960/cleaning-maintenance-guidelines-marshalls-garden-paving-and-driveways.pdf

**Data**: "No more than medium pressure should be applied when washing paving, with the power-washing lance held at an oblique angle to the paving and at least 200mm (8") from the surface." Sprayed diagonally at 30°. Re-sand jointing if washed out.

**Why it matters**: Many DIY guides advise "blast it" and many cheap pro services use turbo / dirt-blaster nozzles for speed. Both violate Marshalls' published cleaning guidance — and Marshalls' "cannot be responsible for the application and performance of any cleaning product" wording means using the wrong technique can compromise any quality claim/warranty argument on Marshalls-branded paving.

- Q-03 can pressure washing damage block paving (primary)

## F-004 — Surrey water cost premium: Thames Water charges ~65p/m³ more than UK average

**Established**: 2026-05-20 (Cycle 1)
**Sources**:
- Thames Water 2026/27 tariff — £4.21/m³ combined
- UK average 2026/27 — ~£3.55-£3.60/m³ combined (water + sewerage)

**Data**: Thames Water's combined rate of £4.21/m³ is ~18% above the UK national average. For a typical 40m² driveway cleaning job using ~1,200L of water, the Surrey water-cost premium adds ~80p per job vs the UK average — small per-job, meaningful over a fleet.
