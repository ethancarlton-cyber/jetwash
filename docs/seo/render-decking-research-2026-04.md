### 2. Why Soft Washing Is Essential for Render

#### The Problem with Pressure Washing Render
- **K-Rend is only ~9mm thick** — taking a few millimetres off each clean leads to cracks, pieces falling off, and uneven finish
- High pressure **strips the surface**, causes cracking, and drives moisture behind the render
- Pressure washing **doesn't kill the root organisms** — spores of moss, algae, and mould remain, meaning stains return within months
- Can cause **etching, surface pitting, and loss of water-repellent properties**
- Documented case: homeowner used standard pressure washer on K-Rend, resulted in **£2,000+ repair and reapplication costs**

**Source**: Leeksons, Nautilus Clean (2025), All Weather Coating, RenWash

#### How Soft Washing Works
1. **Assessment**: Identify growth type — green = algae, black = mould/Gloeocapsa magma, red = Trentepohlia, white = efflorescence
2. **Preparation**: Pre-wet adjacent plants, cover sensitive areas
3. **Chemical application**: Apply biocide solution at low pressure (garden-hose equivalent) using water-fed pole and 12v pump
4. **Dwell time**: Allow chemicals to work:
   - Light organic staining: 10–15 minutes
   - Moderate algae/mould: 20–30 minutes
   - Heavy lichen/black spot: 45–60 minutes
5. **Rinse**: Gentle low-pressure rinse if required
6. **Residual protection**: Biocide continues working for weeks/months after application

**Source**: Benz Softwash, SoftWash UK, Leeksons, AquaPro Cleaning

#### K-Rend's Own Advice
- K-Rend officially advises: "The surface can be cleaned using a pressure washer with clean water and a low concentration of mild detergent" — but on **fan application, not high power**
- K-Rend recommends **light cleaning and maintenance** dependent on weathering and atmospheric spores
- K Pro is K-Rend's own protective treatment — water-based, enhances water-repellent properties

**Source**: K-Rend official website (k-rend.co.uk/training-support/maintenance)

**Source**: Benz Softwash UK, SoftWash UK Ltd, PureSeal Services, AlgoClear

#### How the Chemicals Work
- **DDAC (Didecyldimethylammonium chloride)**: Derived from naturally occurring plant oils. Targets the underlying **biofilm** responsible for algae, lichen, fungi, and mould. Continues working for months after application — "deep cleans over time"
- **Sodium Hypochlorite**: Rapid-acting bleach-based biocide. At 3%–5% active chlorine concentration, penetrates holdfast structures of lichen and rhizoids of moss within 15–30 minutes
- **Combined approach**: Many professionals use SH for initial heavy cleaning, then DDAC for long-term residual protection

**Source**: Benz Softwash, AquaPro Cleaning, National Softwash Authority

#### Treatment Cost to Homeowner
The biocide chemicals themselves are relatively inexpensive — the bulk of professional render cleaning cost covers **labour, equipment, insurance, and expertise**. A typical domestic soft wash uses £10–£30 of chemicals.

### 5. Common Render Problems in the UK

#### Types of Staining
| Stain Type | Organism | Appearance | Common Locations |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Green algae** | Various algae species | Green film or slimy growth | Shaded, damp areas, north-facing walls |
| **Red/orange algae** | Trentepohlia (filamentous green algae with carotenoid pigments) | Reddish-orange streaks or powdery coating | Damp, slightly acidic environments |
| **Black staining** | Gloeocapsa magma (blue-green algae/bacterium) | Black vertical streaks where water runs | Water run-off paths, under gutters |
| **White deposits** | Efflorescence (mineral salts) | White powdery coating | New render, high-moisture areas |

#### Why UK Render Gets Dirty
The UK's **damp, mild climate** is perfect for biological growth. Algae and mould need three things:
1. **Moisture** — frequent rain, dripping gutters, leaking downpipes, splashback from paving
2. **Shade** — north-facing walls, trees, neighbouring buildings
3. **Food source** — organic deposits, pollution, dust

#### Underlying Issues That Accelerate Growth
- Blocked or leaking gutters keeping walls wet longer than normal
- Bad drainage or pooling water at base of walls
- Walls that can't dry out due to shade, poor airflow, or overgrown vegetation
- Metal brackets (satellite dishes, hanging baskets) causing rust staining — K-Rend recommends **stainless steel** fixings only

**Source**: Spectrum Specialist Support, Top Glass Window Cleaning, PureWash SW, AWC Cleaning Supplies, K-Rend

### 8. DIY Render Cleaning Risks

#### Physical Damage
- Homeowners using standard pressure washers cause **etching, surface pitting, and loss of water-repellent properties**
- Render starts absorbing water and discolouring unevenly after damage
- Documented cost of repair: **£2,000+ for reapplication** after pressure washer damage

#### Warranty Implications
- **K-Rend and other modern renders carry manufacturer warranties**
- Using high pressure or unapproved cleaners **will void the warranty**

- Homeowners risk invalidating 10–15 year product warranties with a single improper clean

#### Safety Risks
- Working at height without training or proper equipment
- Chemical handling without PPE or knowledge of dilution ratios
- Risk of damaging windows, UPVC, and adjacent surfaces

**Source**: Leeksons, Nautilus Clean, Exterior Cleaning Solutions, K-Rend official

### 9. Before/After Expectations

#### What Professional Cleaning CAN Fix
- **Green algae**: Usually disappears within 1 hour of treatment
- **Red algae (Trentepohlia)**: Turns pink, then disappears within 4–24 hours
- **Black staining**: Normally disappears within 2 hours after second treatment
- **General weathering/dullness**: Render appears bright and years younger
- **Moss and lichen**: Killed at the root, falls away over days/weeks

#### What Professional Cleaning CANNOT Fix
- **Cracking or structural render damage** — cleaning won't repair physical defects
- **Colour fading from UV damage** — the original pigment may have degraded
- **Damage from previous pressure washing** — etching and pitting are permanent
- **Efflorescence** — may return if underlying moisture issue isn't resolved
- **Rust staining from non-stainless fixings** — requires specific treatment and fixing replacement

#### Important Timing Notes
- Results are **not always instant** — some biological growth clears gradually over weeks
- Deep-rooted black fungi may need multiple treatments
- The surface may **lighten gradually over weeks**, not minutes
- A biocide-treated wall continues to self-clean for months after the professional visit

**Source**: Benz Softwash, Leeksons, WeDoRender, PureWash SW, EcoRend

### 4. Decking Cleaning Methods

#### Pressure Washing
- **Suitable for**: Timber and composite (with correct settings)
- **Pressure settings**: 1,000–1,200 PSI for pressure-treated wood; 500–600 PSI for softwoods like cedar
- **Risks**: Can damage wood fibres, raise grain, cause splintering if too high
- **Cost**: From £150–£350 for professional service

#### Soft Washing / Chemical Treatment
- **Suitable for**: All deck types, especially older/fragile timber
- **Process**: Pre-treat with sodium percarbonate (oxy bleach), power rinse, then treat with brightener/neutraliser
- **Benefits**: Gentler than pressure washing, kills organisms at root
- **Pre-treatment dwell time**: 5–10 minutes for heavily soiled areas

#### Sanding
- **When needed**: After pressure washing (to smooth raised fibres), before re-staining, for heavily weathered boards
- **Process**: Light sanding to remove grey weathered layer, reveal fresh wood beneath
- **Often combined with**: Pressure washing first, then sanding, then oil/stain

#### Full Restoration Process
1. Clear deck of furniture and debris
2. Chemical pre-treatment to kill algae/moss
3. Gentle pressure wash or soft wash
4. Allow to dry (24–48 hours)
5. Light sanding if needed
6. Apply decking oil or stain (1–2 coats)

**Source**: Cladco, Mr Sander, JetReady, Bucks Pressure Washing, R&A Pressure Washing

### 6. How Often UK Decking Needs Cleaning

| Situation | Recommended Frequency |
|-----------|----------------------|
| General maintenance (any deck) | At least **once a year** |
| Best practice | **Twice a year** — spring (pre-summer prep) + autumn (pre-winter clear) |
| High-traffic decks | **3–4 times per year** |
| Shaded / damp areas | **More frequently** — moss and algae build faster |
| Composite decking | **2–3 times per year** — spring, autumn, plus after installation |

- **Spring is the ideal time** — restores deck after winter stresses, prepares for summer use
- Temperatures should be **above 10°C day and night** for treatment application
- Autumn clean clears debris before winter moisture causes rot
- Composite decking still needs cleaning despite being "low maintenance" — soap, hot water, soft-bristle brush

**Source**: Savoy Timber, Karcher, Love The Garden, STIHL, Servos UK, Home Gardener

### 8. Decking Lifespan — With vs Without Maintenance

| Decking Type | WITH Regular Maintenance | WITHOUT Maintenance |
|-------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| **Softwood (pressure treated)** | 10–15 years | 5–10 years |
| **Hardwood** | 25–50 years (species dependent) | 15–20 years |
| **Composite (capped)** | 25–50 years | 20–30 years |
| **Composite (uncapped)** | 15–25 years | 10–15 years |

#### Why Timber Rots Without Maintenance
- UK's temperate maritime climate = prolonged moisture exposure
- Leads to **rot, warping, and splitting**
- High rainfall and coastal exposure accelerate deterioration
- **Most timber decks in the UK reach end of life between year 12 and 18** depending on quality, maintenance, and climate
- At some point, accumulated rot, warping, and structural degradation make it **cheaper to replace than keep patching**
- Full deck replacement essentially **doubles your original investment**

#### Untreated Timber Risks
- No resistance to rot, insects, or moisture damage
- Warping and rotting can begin within 2–3 years without any treatment
- Decking oil seals wood against moisture — without it, water penetrates freely

**Source**: CETA Decking, Timber2uDirect, PGR Timber, Travis Perkins, Duffield Timber, MyDek, TimberX

### 9. Best Time of Year to Clean Decking

| Season | Suitability | Notes |
|--------|------------|-------|
| **Early Spring (March–April)** | **IDEAL** | Restore after winter, prepare for summer. Weather warming above 10°C |
| **Late Spring (May)** | Excellent | Warm enough for oil/stain application to cure properly |
| **Summer (June–August)** | Good | Warm and dry — but deck in heavy use |
| **Early Autumn (September–October)** | Good | Pre-winter clean, clear leaves and debris before wet season |
| **Late Autumn / Winter** | Poor | Too cold for treatments to cure, constant moisture |

- Spring consistently recommended by all professional sources
- Temperature must remain **above 10°C day AND night** for oil/stain application
- Avoid treating just before rain — allow 24–48 hours drying time
- Two-clean cycle (spring + autumn) is best practice for UK decking

**Source**: Savoy Timber, Karcher, STIHL, Furniture Clinic, Servos UK
