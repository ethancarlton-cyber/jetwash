# Lead Pricing Research — What to Charge Per Enquiry

**Date:** 2026-04-18
**Question:** How much should we charge Ben (or any jetwashing tradesman) per enquiry from samedayjetwash.com, and what do competitor lead-gen platforms charge?

---

## TL;DR — Recommended Pricing

| Model | Price | Rationale |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| **Pay-per-lead (shared)** | **£10–£15** per enquiry | Undercuts Checkatrade/Rated People; shared with up to 3 tradesmen |
| **Pay-per-lead (exclusive)** | **£20–£25** per enquiry | Matches Bark/Rated People mid-range; only one tradesman gets it |
| **Monthly retainer (exclusive)** | **£150–£250/month** | Once lead flow is steady (~4+/month). Caps their cost, locks them in |
| **% of job value** | **10%** of quoted job | Aligns incentives, but harder to enforce. Use only with trusted tradesmen |

**Starting recommendation for Ben:** Offer **£15 per exclusive lead** for the first month as a trial. Review after 4–5 leads delivered. If conversion is good, push to £20 or £150/month retainer.

---

## Why these numbers

The lead has to be **cheap enough that one converted job covers many unconverted leads**, but **expensive enough that we earn real money**.

### Tradesman economics (their view)
- Average driveway clean job value: **£150–£400**
- Average patio clean: **£100–£300**
- Industry conversion rate on tradesman-platform leads: **15–30%** (varies wildly)
- At a £15 lead price and 25% conversion → cost per won job = £60 (vs £200 job value = 30% gross margin to marketing, healthy)
- At a £25 lead price and 20% conversion → cost per won job = £125 (vs £250 job value = 50% to marketing, tight but workable)

### Our view
- We pay £0 in ad spend (organic SEO only)
- Each lead is near-100% margin once the site exists
- Even at £10/lead × 4 leads/month = £40/month — this is the proof-of-concept phase
- At £20/lead × 10 leads/month = £200/month — this is the target

## Competitor pricing (what tradesmen already pay)

### Checkatrade
- **Membership:** £50–£120/month (£600–£1,440/year) before a single lead
- **Pay-per-lead add-on:** £5–£18 per lead on top of membership
- **Reality:** Most tradesmen pay **£600+/year just to be listed**, then still compete with other quotes
- **Our angle:** We can offer "exclusive, no membership fee, £15/lead" — cheaper and better quality

### MyBuilder
- **Model:** No membership. Pay only when shortlisted by homeowner.
- **Shortlist fee:** £2–£40 per shortlist depending on job size
- **Problem for tradesmen:** Non-refundable even if they don't win the job
- **Reality:** Tradesmen often pay £100–£300 in fees before winning a single job
- **Our angle:** Our leads are warmer (filled out full quote form, not just browsing)

### Rated People
- **Subscription:** £35/month + VAT (≈£42) including £45 credit
- **Average lead cost:** ~£15 + VAT per contact (≈£18)
- **Shared:** Up to 3 tradesmen contact each homeowner
- **Our angle:** Exclusive leads at similar price point = better value

### Bark.com
- **Credit system:** £1.80 per credit (ex VAT)
- **Leads cost 5–30 credits** = £9–£54 per enquiry
- **Pressure washing leads:** typically 8–15 credits ≈ **£14–£27**
- **Credits expire after 3 months** — pressure to use them
- **Shared:** Multiple Pros contact each lead
- **Our angle:** Exclusive, no expiring credits, straight pay-per-lead

### Local Services Ads (Google LSA)
- $15–$40 per lead (≈£12–£32)
- Requires GBP, licensing checks, reviews
- Not available to us (anonymous model)

## "Credits" — what they actually mean

Most tradesman platforms use credits as a **friction/margin tool**, not a true currency:

- **Bark:** 1 credit = £1.80 ex VAT. Leads priced at 5–30 credits. Credits expire in 3 months.
- **Rated People:** Credits roughly = £1 each. Subscription includes monthly credit allowance.
- **Checkatrade:** No credit system — flat per-lead charge.
- **MyBuilder:** No credit system — direct shortlist fee.

**Why platforms use credits:**
1. Obscures the true £ cost of a lead (psychological)
2. Forces bulk purchase (locks in cash)
3. Expiry creates urgency to use them (drives more leads purchased)

**We should NOT use credits.** Simpler: flat £X per lead, invoiced monthly. Tradesmen strongly prefer transparency over credit systems (consistent feedback on forums).

## Pricing strategy timeline

### Phase 1 — Proof (now → first 10 leads)
- **Offer Ben:** £10/lead (trial rate, first 5 leads)
- **Goal:** Prove conversion rate, get a testimonial/data point
- **Don't tell him yet** — wait for ~1 lead/week sustained. Today's Guildford enquiry is lead #1.

### Phase 2 — Commercial (10+ leads, 1/week sustained)
- **Ben rate:** £15/lead exclusive, or £150/month unlimited (whichever higher for us)
- **Add second tradesman** outside RH1 core (e.g. Crawley-focused) at same rate
- **Goal:** £200–£400/month revenue

### Phase 3 — Scale (50+ leads, 2–3/week)
- **Raise to £20/lead exclusive** or **£250/month retainer**
- **Tiered territories:** Redhill/Reigate premium (£25), outer areas (£15)
- **Goal:** £500–£1,000/month revenue

## What makes OUR leads worth more than Bark/Checkatrade

1. **Exclusive** — only one tradesman gets it (vs 3+ competing quotes elsewhere)
2. **Full form data** — name, postcode, phone, service type, notes (vs "driveway, Redhill")
3. **Hyper-local** — RH1 + 20-mile radius only, all high-intent buyers
4. **No membership fee** — pay only when we deliver
5. **Pay-on-conversion option** — we can offer % of job for trusted partners

## Risk / reality check

- Conversion rate is unknown until we sell a few. **Start cheap, prove, then raise.**
- Ben may push back on "paying for leads from his own mate" — frame it as "covering our costs of running the site" rather than a commercial transaction initially. Or offer first 3 free, then £15 each.
- If we over-price early and he bins it, there's no easy replacement. Under-price by £5 and learn.
