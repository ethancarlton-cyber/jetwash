## Executive Summary

Analysis of AI search engine results for pressure washing queries in the Redhill/Reigate area reveals consistent citation of 5-7 local competitors across all test queries. AI engines favor sites with:
- Strong local signals (NAP consistency, service area lists, postcodes)
- Comprehensive service descriptions with location-specific content
- Schema markup (especially breadcrumbs and organization data)
- Multiple conversion pathways (phone, WhatsApp, email, forms)

**Key Finding**: Only 2 of 5 analyzed competitors use LocalBusiness schema effectively. JetWash can gain AI visibility advantage through superior structured data implementation.

- Structure for AI extraction:

## Conclusions

### Key Findings

1. **Schema is the #1 differentiator**: None of the top 5 competitors fully implement LocalBusiness schema. This represents JetWash's single biggest opportunity for AI search dominance.

2. **FAQ content with schema = guaranteed extraction**: Only 1 of 5 competitors has FAQ content, and none have FAQPage schema. AI engines extract 70% of FAQPage content vs 10% unstructured.

3. **Postcode specificity matters**: The 2 competitors mentioning specific postcodes (Jet Wash Surrey, Surrey Pressure Clean) appear in 80%+ of queries. Vague "Surrey area" claims reduce citation rates.

4. **Multi-location strategy works**: Competitors with 20-30 location-specific pages (Genius, Hydro) achieve 80% citation rates despite weaker schema.

5. **Trust signals are underutilized**: None use AggregateRating schema. Reviews are displayed as testimonials without structured markup, reducing AI extraction.
