Pressure washing across RH4 and RH5

Dorking sits in the bottom of the Mole Valley with the North Downs to the north and the Surrey Hills AONB everywhere else. That sheltered position means rainfall sits longer, the air stays damper, and exterior surfaces grow moss, algae and lichen faster than anywhere else on our patch.

The town centre is mostly Georgian and Victorian, with conservation-area properties along Dene Street, West Street and the High Street. Move out a mile and you’re into 1930s semis along Westcott Road and the Holmwoods, plus larger detached homes around Box Hill, Mickleham and Brockham. Many gardens have York stone or Indian sandstone patios that need soft-washing — full pressure on a soft sandstone leaves it pitted.

We cover Reigate (RH2), Redhill (RH1), Leatherhead (KT22), Betchworth (RH3) and the rest of Surrey within 20 miles. Full list of areas here.

What we see on Dorking drives and patios

  • Heavy moss and lichen — the Mole Valley grows it faster than anywhere else. Pre-treat with biocide, surface-clean, then re-sand joints.
  • Indian sandstone and York stone patios — popular in Box Hill and Westcott gardens. Soft wash only, never high pressure.
  • Painted render — period and modern. Algae streaks under gutters within two seasons; soft-washed off without stripping the coating.
  • Long rural driveways — common out in Mickleham and Brockham. Commercial-grade kit so 80m² doesn’t take all day.

Dorking FAQs

Most-asked questions from RH4 and RH5 customers.

How much does pressure washing cost in Dorking?

Most Dorking driveways and patios come in between £100 and £300. Period properties with Indian sandstone or larger rural drives sit at the higher end. Free no-obligation quote on any RH4 or RH5 property.

Do you cover Box Hill, Westcott and Holmwood?

Yes — all of RH4 and RH5 including Box Hill, Westcott, North and South Holmwood, Brockham, Mickleham and Betchworth. Twelve miles west of our Redhill base, well within our 20-mile radius.

Can you clean natural stone patios in the Dorking area?

Yes — Indian sandstone, York stone and slate are common in Dorking gardens. We use low-pressure soft wash with biocide, never blasting at full power. The stone keeps its surface texture and colour.

Why does Dorking get so much moss and algae?

The Mole Valley is sheltered by the North Downs and the Surrey Hills, so rain sits longer and surfaces stay damp. Properties on north-facing slopes or near woodland get the worst of it. Annual or biannual cleaning keeps it under control.
