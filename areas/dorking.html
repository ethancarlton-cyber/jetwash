Why Dorking Properties Need Regular Cleaning

The local environment creates specific challenges for Dorking homeowners:

  • Heavy moss and lichen growth: The sheltered Mole Valley position, combined with mature trees throughout the town's conservation areas, creates heavy shade and damp conditions ideal for aggressive moss and lichen growth
  • Natural stone care requirements: Dorking's period and character properties often feature York stone, Indian sandstone, or local stone patios that need pH-neutral cleaners and controlled pressure to avoid etching and discolouration
  • Leaf staining and organic debris: Properties near the Deepdene Trail, Ranmore Common, and Box Hill experience significant leaf fall that stains driveways and patios with tannins if not swept regularly
  • Render and painted surfaces: Both older and newer Dorking properties frequently have rendered walls that attract green algae, requiring soft wash treatment rather than high-pressure cleaning
  • Rural property access: Properties in outlying villages like Westcott, Mickleham, and Brockham sometimes have longer driveways and more extensive paved areas requiring commercial-grade equipment
