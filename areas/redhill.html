Pressure washing across RH1

Redhill and the surrounding RH1 villages — Merstham, Salfords, Earlswood, Nutfield, South Nutfield — sit on a particular geology that affects how driveways and patios age. The North Downs catch the weather coming off the M25, drives stay damp longer than they would 10 miles south, and that means moss in the joints of every block-paving drive after about two winters without a clean.

Most of the work in RH1 is mid-eighties to early-2000s block-paving driveways — standard 200×100×50 mm blocks with kiln-dried sand joints that have long since been washed out. The job we do most often: pre-treat the moss with biocide, surface-clean the slabs, hand-detail the kerb lines, then re-sand the joints so the next two winters don’t set the cycle going again.

Reigate (RH2) is right next door and we cover that as well, plus Horley to the south, Banstead and Caterham over the hill. Full list of areas here.

What we see on Redhill drives

  • Block paving with washed-out joints — almost universal in RH1 estates built 1985-2005. Re-sanding included with every clean.
  • Indian sandstone patios — popular in Reigate Hill and Redstone Hill. Black-spot lichen is the classic Redhill problem; soft wash with biocide handles it.
  • Painted render — a lot of newer-build RH1 homes. Algae streaks under gutters are the giveaway. Soft wash, no pressure damage.
  • Concrete driveways — older Merstham and Salfords stock. Oil staining is the issue, pre-treatment plus pressure washing handles most of it.
