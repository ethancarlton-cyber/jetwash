Pressure washing across RH3

Betchworth is five miles west of Redhill, in the Mole Valley between us and Dorking. The village is one of Surrey’s prettier conservation areas — period properties, Grade II listed cottages, and substantial country homes around Brockham, Buckland and Strood Green.

The Mole Valley microclimate makes Betchworth a particularly damp posting. The North Downs shelter the village from drying winds, and the River Mole keeps humidity levels high year-round. Combined with mature trees and dense hedgerows, that means moss, algae and lichen colonise exterior surfaces faster than almost anywhere else on our patch.

Most of the work here needs a heritage-aware approach: soft wash on period stonework, lime render and old brick, never high-pressure blasting that would strip the surface. We cover Reigate (RH2), Dorking (RH4), Leigh, Gadbrook and the rest of Surrey within 20 miles of Redhill. Full list of areas here.

What we see on Betchworth drives and patios

  • Rapid moss growth — the Mole Valley speciality. The Street, Wonham Lane, around Brockham Green — all the worst.
  • Lichen on natural stone — Surrey sandstone and flint walls and paths. Soft wash with biocide, never high pressure.
  • Algae on lime render and painted walls — conservation-area property. Soft wash exclusively; high pressure removes render entirely.
  • Slippery green patios — valley humidity. Indian sandstone takes the worst of it.
