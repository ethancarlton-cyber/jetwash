Common Driveway Problems in Oxted

Oxted homeowners regularly contact us about these issues:

  • Moss and lichen on natural stone: Many older Oxted properties have natural stone driveways and paths that develop thick moss and lichen colonies. The rural setting and mature tree cover create ideal damp, shaded conditions for this growth
  • Leaf tannin staining: Heavy autumn leaf fall from oak, beech, and horse chestnut trees leaves dark brown tannin stains on paving that cannot be removed with standard washing. Professional treatment is needed to restore the original colour
  • Green algae on long driveways: Longer rural driveways, common in the RH8 area, develop green algae growth particularly along shaded edges and under overhanging branches. This creates a slip hazard, especially after rain
  • Mud and clay deposits: Properties near farm tracks and rural lanes collect Wealden clay deposits that are tracked onto driveways by vehicles. This orange-brown clay staining is stubborn and needs pressure washing to remove properly
  • Weathered Indian sandstone patios: Sandstone is a popular patio material in Oxted but requires careful cleaning to avoid surface damage. Incorrect DIY pressure washing can leave permanent marks on this softer stone
