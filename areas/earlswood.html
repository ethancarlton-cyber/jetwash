Common Driveway Problems in Earlswood

Earlswood homeowners regularly contact us about these issues:

  • Heavy tree coverage causing moss and algae: The defining issue in Earlswood. Dense canopy from mature trees along residential streets creates persistent shade that encourages thick moss growth on block paving, concrete, and tarmac driveways. North-facing properties near the Common are particularly affected, with moss colonising surfaces within months of cleaning
  • Leaf tannin staining from lakeside trees: Properties near Earlswood Lakes and the Common experience heavy autumn leaf fall from oak, beech, and birch trees. Decomposing leaves leach dark brown tannins into porous surfaces like sandstone and concrete, leaving deep stains that rainwater alone cannot remove. These tannin marks become more pronounced year on year without treatment
  • Green algae on shaded patios: Many Earlswood gardens are surrounded by tall hedging and mature trees that restrict airflow and sunlight. This creates ideal conditions for green algae films to develop on patio slabs, making surfaces dangerously slippery when wet. Natural stone patios are especially susceptible in these shaded garden settings. Our guide on the best time for patio cleaning can help you plan maintenance around Earlswood's seasonal conditions
  • Block paving moss near the Common: The proximity to Earlswood Common means high moisture levels and abundant organic matter. Block paving driveways in the streets surrounding the Common develop thick moss in the joints, pushing out kiln-dried sand and allowing weeds to establish. Without regular cleaning and re-sanding, blocks become unstable and the surface deteriorates
  • Dirty render on period properties: Many of Earlswood's older properties have painted render or pebble dash that attracts green algae streaks, particularly on north and west-facing elevations. The combination of tree shade and moisture from the surrounding landscape means render can turn green within a single season, making the whole property look neglected
