Pressure washing across Merstham

Merstham is three miles north of Redhill at the foot of the North Downs, sitting right on the M25/M23 interchange. The village has a split character: period Quality Street and the older village fabric on one side, and 1960s-2000s estates in South Merstham closer to the motorway corridor on the other.

Motorway pollution defines the work here. Properties within a mile of the junction accumulate a dark, greasy film from traffic exhaust, brake dust and tyre particles. It bonds to porous surfaces — concrete and block paving especially — and won’t come off with a hose. We use commercial degreasers and hot water to lift it. Add chalk dust from the Downs and the mature tree shade along Quality Street and Dean Lane, and Merstham surfaces deteriorate faster than the rest of RH1.

We cover Redhill town centre, Reigate (RH2), Caterham (CR3) and the rest of Surrey within 20 miles. Full list of areas here.

What we see on Merstham drives

  • M25/M23 pollution film — the Merstham speciality. Hot water plus commercial degreaser is the only way to lift it.
  • Chalk dust deposits — pale powdery coating on dark surfaces. Mixes with rain to form a cement-like residue.
  • Heavy moss along Quality Street — mature tree shade plus damp escarpment. Soft wash on period stonework, never high pressure.
  • Block paving in South Merstham — 80s and 90s estates with deteriorated joint sand. Surface clean plus re-sanding.

Can you remove motorway pollution stains from driveways?

Yes — this is the defining Merstham job. Hot water pressure washing plus commercial degreasers breaks down traffic film, oil deposits and exhaust residue. Older staining responds slower but improves significantly.

How do you handle moss on period properties around Quality Street?

Soft wash — low pressure plus biocidal solutions that kill moss at the root without damaging historic stonework, brick or pointing. Lasts 12-18 months.
