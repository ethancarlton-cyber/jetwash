Common Driveway Problems in Merstham

Merstham homeowners regularly contact us about these issues:

  • Motorway pollution staining: The most distinctive problem in Merstham. Properties within a mile of the M25/M23 junction accumulate a dark, greasy film from traffic exhaust and tyre particles. This pollution bonds to porous surfaces like concrete and block paving, requiring commercial degreasers and hot water treatment to remove oil and grease stains effectively
  • Chalk dust deposits: The North Downs chalk geology means fine white dust settles on surfaces throughout the village, particularly during dry spells. This creates a pale, powdery coating on driveways and patios that mixes with rain to form a stubborn, cement-like residue
  • Heavy moss from North Downs shade: The escarpment and mature tree canopy along Quality Street, Dean Lane, and Gatton Bottom create heavily shaded conditions. Moss colonises block paving joints, concrete paths, and patio slabs within months, making surfaces slippery and unsightly. See our guide on effective moss removal techniques for more information
  • Leaf debris from mature trees: Merstham's established gardens and tree-lined streets produce heavy leaf fall each autumn. Decomposing leaves release tannins that stain lighter surfaces brown, and wet leaf debris creates ideal conditions for algae growth beneath
  • Block paving weed invasion: Many of the 1980s and 1990s estates in South Merstham have block paving driveways where jointing sand has deteriorated over decades. Weeds, grass, and moss colonise the gaps, destabilising blocks and spoiling the appearance
