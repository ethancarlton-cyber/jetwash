Common Driveway Problems in Caterham

Caterham homeowners regularly contact us about these issues:

  • Heavy moss on steep driveways: Caterham's hillside location means many driveways are sloped, creating drainage channels where moisture lingers. North-facing slopes in Caterham on the Hill are particularly prone to thick moss growth that becomes dangerously slippery
  • Leaf staining on patios: The mature woodland surrounding Caterham drops heavy leaf fall each autumn. Tannin staining from decaying leaves creates dark marks on paving slabs and natural stone that standard hosing cannot remove
  • Algae on shaded valley driveways: Properties in Caterham Valley sit lower and receive less direct sunlight, creating ideal conditions for green algae that coats driveways and paths in a slippery film
  • Chalk dust and mineral deposits: The North Downs chalk geology means Caterham's water supply is hard, leaving white mineral deposits on surfaces. Combined with chalk dust, driveways develop a dull, powdery appearance
  • Block paving weed invasion: Weeds push through jointing sand on block paving driveways, destabilising the surface. The damp conditions across the CR3 area accelerate weed growth between blocks
