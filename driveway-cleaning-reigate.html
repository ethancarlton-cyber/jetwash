Block paving, tarmac, concrete and resin drives in Reigate

Reigate straddles two very different bits of ground within a ten-minute walk of the High Street. North of town, Reigate Hill, Wray Lane and Wray Common sit on the free-draining North Downs Chalk; south of town, Woodhatch, South Park and Dovers Green sit on Lower Greensand and Weald Clay that holds its moisture[1]. Add the mature beech and oak of the North Downs shading the drives up the Hill, and you get the same pattern every time: the shaded half greens over first. What that means for each surface:

  • Block paving on the estates. Woodhatch, South Park and Dovers Green are wall-to-wall block paving. The joints are the weak point: sand washes out, weeds and moss move in, and the whole drive looks tired even where the bricks are sound. On the clay-and-greensand side those joints empty faster, so re-sanding earns its keep. We wash, kill the weeds and re-sand with kiln dried sand as standard. More on the surface itself on our block paving cleaning page.
  • Big sweeping drives up the Hill. Reigate Hill and Wray Common detached homes often run to 60–90m² of block paving or resin, shaded by beech and oak. The chalk drains well, but the tree canopy keeps the surface damp so biofilm builds before the drive even looks dirty. We bring commercial-grade kit so a big drive does not take all day.
  • Concrete and tarmac in the town centre. The Victorian and Edwardian streets around the Old Town, West Street and Chart Lane mostly have small concrete or tarmac forecourts, some inside the conservation area. Quick jobs, usually at or near the £80 minimum, and the difference between grey-black and actually-its-original-colour is startling.
  • Resin-bound drives. A few of the newer Skimmington and Reigate Heath builds have resin. It cleans up well but hates high pressure up close, the binder can scar, so we run lower pressure and more chemistry. We price these on a photo or a quick look first.

What’s included in a Reigate driveway clean

  1. Walk the drive first. Surface, condition, which side of the High Street you are on, drainage, oil stains, anything loose or cracked. If a repair is what you actually need, we say so rather than wash over the problem.
  2. Pre-treat. Moss and algae get a biocide treatment before the water goes anywhere near it, with a longer dwell on the shaded north-facing drives up the Hill. The dilution and dwell time do the work, not brute pressure.
  3. Machine wash. Rotary surface cleaner across the field of the drive for an even, stripe-free finish, then the lance for edges, corners and the awkward bits by the garage door.
  4. Weeds and joints. Weeds cleared from the joints, and on block paving we re-sand with kiln dried sand once the surface has dried off. Included, always, and doubly worth it on the Woodhatch and South Park clay side where joints wash out fastest.
  5. Tidy up. Slurry and debris bagged and taken away, paths and gullies rinsed down. We leave the drive clean and the rest of the frontage no worse than we found it.

Optional extras, priced honestly: oil stain treatment (some stains never fully lift from concrete, we’ll tell you before we start, and our oil stain guide shows what’s realistic) and sealing after the clean. Half of Surrey drives don’t need sealing at all, and on the damp clay side south of the High Street a sealer trapped over surface moisture can blister, so we’ll only quote it where it earns its keep.

Why does my Reigate driveway keep greening over so fast?

The North Downs tree line does it. Drives shaded by the mature beech and oak along Reigate Hill and Wray Common stay damp for longer, and damp is what algae and moss want, so the shaded half greens over first even on the free-draining chalk. South of the High Street the Weald Clay and greensand hold surface moisture, so joints wash out and moss returns quicker again.

Do you re-sand block paving joints after cleaning?

Yes, always, with kiln dried sand, and it is in the price. Clean bricks with washed-out joints look worse than dirty bricks with full joints, and empty joints let the weeds straight back in. Joints wash out faster on the Woodhatch and South Park side, so re-sanding matters most there. If you want the drive sealed afterwards we price that separately and tell you straight if your paving does not need it.
