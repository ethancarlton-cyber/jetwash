Block paving, resin, tarmac and concrete drives in Banstead

Banstead sits on top of the North Downs, on the Upper Cretaceous Chalk that the British Geological Survey maps across the whole SM7 ridge[1]. Chalk is highly permeable, so rainwater drains straight down rather than pooling under a sub-base. That is good news for the paving structure: SM7 drives rarely show the water-lift problems you get on the clay further north. The catch is the tree cover. Banstead Woods, the Kingswood Warren treelines and the mature gardens through Nork and Chipstead throw deep shade, and shade plus a bit of diffuse damp is all algae and moss need. What that means surface by surface:

  • Block paving on the estates. Nork, Kingswood and the newer SM7 developments are wall-to-wall block paving. The joints are the weak point: sand washes out, weeds and moss move in, and the whole drive looks tired even where the bricks are sound. We wash, kill the weeds and re-sand with kiln dried sand as standard. More on the surface itself on our block paving cleaning page.
  • Big resin-bound drives. A lot of the Kingswood and Tadworth rebuilds went resin. It cleans up beautifully but hates high pressure up close, the binder can scar, so we run lower pressure and more chemistry. We price these on a photo or a quick look first.
  • Concrete and tarmac on the older streets. The village fabric around Banstead High Street and parts of Woodmansterne still has concrete and tarmac forecourts. Quick jobs, often near the £80 minimum, and the jump from grey-black to original colour is startling.
  • The shaded half greens first. On almost every SM7 drive the north-facing or tree-shaded side is visibly worse than the sunny side. That is the chalk-drains-fast, trees-hold-damp trade-off in one photo. It all comes off the same, and an annual biocide keeps the shaded band from racing back.

What’s included in a Banstead driveway clean

  1. Walk the drive first. Surface, condition, drainage, oil stains, any sunken bays or loose blocks. On block paving we check whether the paving is on a Marshalls Register or similar warranty, because that dictates pressure and technique[2]. If a repair is what you actually need, we say so rather than wash over it.
  2. Pre-treat. Moss and algae get a biocide treatment before the water goes anywhere near it. The dilution and dwell time do the work, not brute pressure. On the shaded SM7 drives this is the step that actually earns its keep.
  3. Machine wash. Rotary surface cleaner across the field of the drive for an even, stripe-free finish, then the lance for edges, corners and the awkward bits by the garage. Medium pressure at an oblique angle on block paving, in line with Marshalls guidance[2], never a turbo nozzle that can void the warranty.
  4. Weeds and joints. Weeds cleared from the joints, and on block paving we re-sand with kiln dried sand once the surface has dried off. Included, always.
  5. Tidy up. Slurry and debris bagged and taken away, paths and gullies rinsed down. We leave the drive clean and the rest of the frontage no worse than we found it.

Optional extras, priced honestly: oil stain treatment (some stains never fully lift from concrete, we’ll tell you before we start, and our moss removal guide covers the recurring-moss side in detail) and sealing after the clean. Plenty of Surrey drives don’t need sealing at all, so we’ll only quote it where it earns its keep.
