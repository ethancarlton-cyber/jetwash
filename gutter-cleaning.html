How a gutter clean works

Four steps. Most homes done inside 2 hours.

Inspect

Walk the perimeter, check every gutter run, identify blockages and any obvious damage. Note anything that’ll need flagging.

Hand-clear

Ladder access, hand-clear leaves, moss, silt and the bird’s nest you didn’t know about. Bagged, not dropped on your borders.

Flush downpipes

Hose down each downpipe to confirm it’s running clear to the drain. Most overflow problems are downpipe blockages, not gutters.

Quick fixes & report

Reseat loose brackets, reseal leaking joints, flag anything beyond a quick fix. You get an honest report of what we found.

How we clean your gutters

Ground-based vacuum where we can, hands-on where we must.

Ground-based gutter vacuum

A high-reach wet-and-dry vacuum on a carbon-fibre pole reaches gutters up to around 12m (40ft) from the ground, so there’s no ladder leaning on your wall. Safer on the steep streets around Redhill and Reigate, and a lot kinder to period and 3-storey terraces.

Hands-on for the stubborn bits

Compacted moss mats, silt that’s set like concrete, the bird’s nest in the corner, some things a vacuum won’t pull through. We go up the ladder and clear those by hand. Most jobs end up a hybrid of vacuum plus hands-on.

Downpipe flush to the drain

We hose every downpipe and confirm the water actually reaches the main drain. That’s the part most cleans skip, and it’s the usual reason gutters overflow again a few weeks later.

Working from the ground keeps us inside the Work at Height Regulations 2005, which say avoid working at height where it’s reasonably practicable. Honestly: clearing a gutter off a ladder isn’t a job worth risking your neck over.

Why gutter cleaning must be done right

An overflowing gutter doesn’t just look bad, it pours water down the outside wall every time it rains. A decent downpipe carries water away at speed, so once it backs up the gutter spills over the front edge by the litre. Six months of that and you’ve got penetrating damp patches on the inside wall, rotting fascia behind the gutter, and rendered walls staining green from constant wet. £75 once a year prevents a £400 fascia repair and a £1,000 damp-treatment bill.

Doing it properly is mostly about access, not muscle. The Work at Height Regulations 2005 require you to avoid working at height where reasonably practicable, which is exactly why we run a ground-based gutter vacuum on a carbon pole to around 12m and only go up a ladder for the stubborn bits. The other half of doing it right is the downpipe: clear the visible run but leave a plugged downpipe and the gutter overflows again within weeks of the next heavy rain. We hose every downpipe and confirm flow to the main drain before we pack up, so the clean actually lasts the year.

Common mistakes with gutter cleaning

Most of the gutter jobs we get called back to fix went wrong in one of four ways:

  • Skipping the downpipe. The single most common one. Clear every gutter run perfectly but leave a silted downpipe, and the water backs up and spills over again in the next downpour. We flush each downpipe and confirm it’s running to the drain.
  • Cleaning too early in autumn. Clear the gutters in early October and you’ll get a fresh load dumped on top through November. In Surrey the sweet spot is late October to December, once the bulk of the leaf-fall is down.
  • Balancing on a ladder with no insurance. The Work at Height Regulations 2005 say avoid working at height where it’s reasonably practicable, and falls from ladders are exactly why. If a contractor falls on your property uninsured, that’s your problem, not just theirs.
  • Pressure-washing the gutters from above. Blasting debris off the roof into an already-blocked gutter just shoves the silt into the downpipe and packs it harder. Gutters get hand-cleared and vacuumed, not jet-blasted.

Signs your gutters need clearing

Water cascading off the side

The most obvious one. If water’s coming over the front edge of the gutter during rain, it’s blocked. Don’t leave it, that’s a wall-damaging amount of water.

Green stains down the wall

Algae growing on the wall directly below a gutter joint = constant slow leak. Joint needs resealing, gutter needs clearing.

Plants growing in the gutter

Always funny when you spot it. Means there’s enough silt for seedlings to root, the gutter’s fully blocked, has been for a while.

Sagging gutter sections

Weight of trapped water and silt pulling the gutter away from the fascia. Bracket fix while we’re up there.

Gutter cleaning FAQs

Most-asked questions from Surrey customers.

How much does gutter cleaning cost in the UK?

Gutter cleaning costs £75–£150 in 2026 depending on property type. A terrace runs about £75, a semi around £100, and a detached around £150. Roof height, gutter length and access drive the price, and Surrey and the South East tend to sit 10–15% above the UK average.

How much is gutter cleaning for a 3 bed semi?

A standard 3-bed semi-detached is roughly £75–£100 with us, and £80–£120 is the typical UK band. It usually takes 45–60 minutes including the downpipe flush. The price moves with gutter length, how much compacted moss is up there, and whether the back is easy to reach.

How much does gutter cleaning with scaffolding cost?

Scaffold is rarely needed, most homes are reachable from the ground with a high-reach vacuum. Where genuine access problems force a tower or scaffold (awkward 3-storey or extension valleys), expect £150–£300+, since the access hire is the real cost. We’ll always try a ground-based clean first before quoting scaffold.

What are the signs you need your gutters cleaned?

Water cascading off the side in rain, green algae streaks down the wall under the gutter, plants or moss growing in the gutter, sagging sections, and damp patches inside near the roofline. Any one of those means the gutters are overdue and the wall behind is starting to take water.

Can blocked gutters cause damp?

Yes. Blocked gutters are one of the leading causes of damp in UK homes. When the gutter overflows, water runs straight down the wall and saturates the brickwork and mortar, and over time that moisture penetrates inside as damp patches and mould. It also rots the fascia and erodes ground near the foundations.

How often should gutters be cleaned?

Once a year for most Surrey homes, ideally late autumn after the leaves have dropped. If you’re surrounded by trees, conifers or have a shaded north-facing roof, step up to twice a year, spring and autumn. Tree-canopy proximity, not rainfall, is what drives blockages here.

Do you flush downpipes too?

Yes, every clean. Blocked downpipes cause more overflow than blocked gutters. We confirm each one’s running clear to the drain.

Do you use ladders or a gutter vacuum?

Ground-based gutter vacuum where we can, no ladders on your wall, safer on steep streets. We switch to hands-on from a ladder for compacted moss or a downpipe blockage the vacuum can’t pull through. Most jobs are a hybrid.

Will you make a mess of my garden?

No, we bag the debris and take it away rather than scattering it over your borders. With the vacuum most of it goes straight into the machine.
