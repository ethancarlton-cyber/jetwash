Block paving, tarmac, concrete and resin drives in Redhill

Redhill sits on the floor of the Vale of Holmesdale, the low corridor between the North Downs Chalk and the Lower Greensand ridge, a boundary the British Geological Survey maps straight through the town[1]. Valley-floor air holds its damp, and damp is what algae and moss want. Drive up onto the chalk at Banstead and driveways stay drier for longer; down here the shaded half of a drive greens over first, every time. What that means for each surface:

  • Block paving on the estates. Merstham, Salfords and the newer RH1 developments are wall-to-wall block paving. The joints are the weak point: sand washes out, weeds and moss move in, and the whole drive looks tired even where the bricks are sound. We wash, kill the weeds and re-sand with kiln dried sand as standard. More on the surface itself on our block paving cleaning page.
  • Concrete and tarmac on the older streets. The Victorian terraces off Redstone Hill and the streets near the station mostly have small concrete or tarmac forecourts. Quick jobs, usually at or near the £80 minimum, and the difference between grey-black and actually-its-original-colour is startling.
  • Resin-bound drives. A few of the newer builds have resin. It cleans up well but hates high pressure up close, the binder can scar, so we run lower pressure and more chemistry. We price these on a photo or a quick look first.
  • Sloped drives on the hill. Redhill isn’t flat. The drives climbing Redstone Hill drain fast on the top half and hold water at the bottom, so the moss concentrates in a band near the pavement. Normal, and it comes off the same.

What’s included in a Redhill driveway clean

  1. Walk the drive first. Surface, condition, drainage, oil stains, anything loose or cracked. If a repair is what you actually need, we say so rather than wash over the problem.
  2. Pre-treat. Moss and algae get a sodium hypochlorite treatment before the water goes anywhere near it. The dilution and dwell time do the work, not brute pressure.
  3. Machine wash. Rotary surface cleaner across the field of the drive for an even, stripe-free finish, then the lance for edges, corners and the awkward bits by the garage door.
  4. Weeds and joints. Weeds cleared from the joints, and on block paving we re-sand with kiln dried sand once the surface has dried off. Included, always.
  5. Tidy up. Slurry and debris bagged and taken away, paths and gullies rinsed down. We leave the drive clean and the rest of the frontage no worse than we found it.

Optional extras, priced honestly: oil stain treatment (some stains never fully lift from concrete, we’ll tell you before we start, and our oil stain guide shows what’s realistic) and sealing after the clean. Half of Surrey drives don’t need sealing at all, so we’ll only quote it where it earns its keep.

How long does cleaning a 50 square metre drive take?

Two to three hours for most Redhill drives. Pre-treat, wash with the surface cleaner, lance the edges and corners, then clear up. Block paving takes longer because the joints need re-sanding with kiln dried sand once the surface has dried, on a wet week that can mean a short return visit.

Do you re-sand block paving after cleaning?

Yes, always, with kiln dried sand, and it is in the price. Clean bricks with washed-out joints look worse than dirty bricks with full joints, and empty joints let the weeds straight back in. If you want the drive sealed afterwards we will price that separately and tell you straight if your paving does not need it.
