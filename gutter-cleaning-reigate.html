What’s in every Reigate gutter clean

A downpipe flush means running water down each vertical pipe until it discharges freely at the drain — it clears the bends where silt and leaf-mulch set hard and cause the overflow, which is the step the cheap adverts skip. We do it on every downpipe, every time, at no extra charge. The gutter vacuum is a ground-based wet/dry vacuum on a carbon-fibre pole rated to around 12m (40ft) of working height (the reach of a SkyVac Atom-class system), so high gutters are cleared from the ground rather than off a ladder.

Why blocked gutters cost Reigate homes more

An overflowing gutter pours water down the outside wall every time it rains. On a Reigate Hill detached with painted render that’s a fast route to green streaking, rotting fascia behind the gutter, and damp patches showing up on the inside wall near the roofline — the same persistent dampness UK government guidance links to mould and the respiratory health risks that come with it. A £75–£120 clean once or twice a year heads off a fascia or soffit replacement and a far bigger render or damp bill. It’s the cheapest preventative job on the whole house.

How often in Reigate? Usually twice.

For an open-aspect home in the Old Town or Woodhatch, once a year after leaf-fall is plenty. But if you’re on the Wray Lane or Reigate Hill side under mature beech and oak — and a lot of RH2 is — make it twice: a spring clear after the catkins and an autumn clear after the leaves come down.
